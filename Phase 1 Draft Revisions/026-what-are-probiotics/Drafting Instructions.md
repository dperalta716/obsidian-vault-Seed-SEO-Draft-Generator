# Strategic Analysis Context

## Seed SciCare POV Summary

### Key Seed Perspectives Found
- **Fermented foods ≠ probiotics**: Seed distinguishes "Live Dietary Microbes (LDM)" from probiotics scientifically. Fermented foods contain varying amounts of microbes with no defined dose and no demonstrated specific benefits — they do not meet the internationally recognized definition of a probiotic. The current article already covers this well.
- **Probiotics are transient, not colonizing**: Seed emphasizes that probiotics do not take up permanent residence in the gut. They work through interactions (immune cells, gut cells, dietary nutrients, existing bacteria) during their transit, not by reshaping the microbiome composition. This is a core differentiator from industry messaging.
- **Acclimation period framing**: Seed frames initial side effects as a "temporary acclimation period" confined to the GI system (changes to stool patterns, mild bloating/gas). This is NOT "die-off" or "Herxheimer reaction" — Seed considers die-off claims scientifically unsubstantiated for probiotics.
- **Strain specificity is paramount**: Benefits are assessed at strain AND dosage level. One strain's benefits cannot be assumed to apply to another strain, even within the same species. This is a key Seed differentiator from competitors who make genus-level claims.
- **AFU is "more precise" (not "more accurate") than CFU**: Specific compliance language requirement.

### Conflicts Identified
- **Competitors casually list fermented foods as "probiotic foods"**: Cleveland Clinic, Harvard, WebMD, and NIH all list yogurt, kefir, kimchi, etc. as probiotic sources without distinction. Seed's position is these are fermented foods, not probiotics — they may contain beneficial microbes but haven't demonstrated strain-specific benefits at defined doses.
- **Competitors make genus-level benefit claims**: Competitors say "Lactobacillus helps with diarrhea" or "Bifidobacterium eases IBS." Seed insists benefits are strain-specific, not genus-level.
- **Competitors mention "colonization" language**: Some competitors imply probiotics restore or rebalance the gut by colonizing it. Seed's stance is probiotics are transient and don't colonize.
- **"Boost immunity" language**: Competitors use phrases like "boost your immune system." This is a no-no phrase for Seed — must use "support immune function" or similar.

### Topics to Handle Carefully
- **Safety/side effects**: Frame as "temporary acclimation period" per SciCare POV. Do NOT use "die-off" or "detox reaction" language.
- **Condition-specific claims**: DS-01 has been studied for specific benefits (digestive health, gut barrier, etc.) but NOT for all conditions competitors discuss (e.g., SIBO, Crohn's disease). Avoid implying DS-01 treats conditions it hasn't been studied for.
- **Never refer to probiotics as "supplements"**: Per compliance rules, Seed avoids this term for probiotics.
