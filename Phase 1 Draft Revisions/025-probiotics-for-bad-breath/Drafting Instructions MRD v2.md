## 3. Formatting Instructions

### Bolding
- Bold key terms on first use (e.g., **volatile sulfur compounds (VSCs)**, **bacteriocins**, **organoleptic test**)
- Bold strain names on first mention (e.g., ***Streptococcus salivarius* K12**)
- Bold practical takeaways and actionable advice

### Citation Format
- In-text: `([Author Year](DOI_URL))` -- no comma between author and year
- Multiple: `([Author1 Year1](DOI_URL1), [Author2 Year2](DOI_URL2))`
- Full APA list at end, numbered, alphabetical by author last name

### Source Hierarchy
1. **Studies from our existing citations** (retain all 11 current sources unless replacing with better versions)
2. **2025 systematic review** (Chandramoorthy et al.) -- highest priority new addition
3. **Additional peer-reviewed sources** to fill specific gaps
4. **Target: 13-15 total sources** (currently 11; add 2-4 new ones)

### Citation Misattribution Guard
For every claim with a citation, the drafter must verify:
- The cited study actually supports the specific claim being made
- The study population matches what is being discussed (healthy adults vs. clinical population)
- In vitro findings are labeled as such, not presented as human clinical results
- Animal studies are not cited as if they were human trials

### Recommendation 10: Cite 2025 Systematic Review

**Priority:** HIGH
**Placement:** Multiple touchpoints -- (a) "Can Probiotics Help?" intro paragraph, (b) strain discussion, (c) safety section, (d) FAQ answers
**MRD Compliance:** Frame as general science.

**Content Guidance:**
This is the most comprehensive and recent evidence synthesis available. It should be woven throughout the article as a backbone reference. Currently our article relies on individual studies; anchoring to a systematic review strengthens credibility.
