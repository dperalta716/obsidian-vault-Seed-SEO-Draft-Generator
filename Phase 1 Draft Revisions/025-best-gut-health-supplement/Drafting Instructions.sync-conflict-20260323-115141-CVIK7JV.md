# Strategic Analysis Context

## Seed SciCare POV Summary

### Key Seed Perspectives Found

- **Fermented foods are NOT probiotics**: Seed distinguishes "live dietary microbes" (LDM) from probiotics. Fermented foods lack strain identification, dosage verification, and demonstrated health benefits. The article currently handles this well (citation 16, Marco 2021) but could strengthen the LDM distinction per Seed's SciCare POV.
- **Probiotics are transient, not colonizing**: Science supports that probiotics do not colonize the gut long-term. They work through specific interactions with resident microbes and local cells. Benefits are generally confined to the period of intake. The article correctly states "most don't permanently colonize your gut" but could emphasize this more as a differentiator.
- **Strain specificity is foundational**: Benefits are clinically assessed and demonstrated at the strain level. Data from one strain cannot be applied to another. Seed uses the dog breed analogy: "just like a golden retriever and a french bulldog are incredibly different, these strains of bacteria can also vary significantly."
- **AFU is "more precise" (not "more accurate") than CFU**: Specific compliance language requirement.
- **Never call probiotics "supplements"**: The article's H1 title uses "Supplements" which is SEO-driven (the keyword is "best gut health supplement"), but body content should minimize calling probiotics specifically "supplements" — Seed prefers "synbiotic" for DS-01.

### Conflicts Identified

- **Competitors casually recommend fermented foods as "probiotic foods"**: This contradicts Seed's scientific position that fermented foods are LDMs, not probiotics. The current article handles this reasonably with the phrase "even if they don't meet the strict definition of probiotics" — but could go further.
- **Competitors use "boost immunity" language**: Forbidden by Seed compliance. Use "support immune function" or "support immune health" instead. The current article uses "nudging immune responses" which is compliant.
- **Competitors frame "gut imbalance" as a condition**: Seed's position is that "imbalance" is not a formally recognized condition. Use "disrupted by factors like diet, medications, or stress."
- **Die-off/Herxheimer language**: Some competitors reference die-off reactions. Seed considers this "inaccurate/misleading; not a well-documented response to probiotics." The current article does not use this language (good).

### Topics to Handle Carefully

- **IBD/IBS claims**: DS-01 has not been studied in individuals with SIBO. IBD evidence is inconclusive. Any condition-specific claims must be heavily hedged.
- **Postbiotics**: If mentioned, many commercial products claiming postbiotics "fall short of the actual definition." Be precise with ISAPP definition.
- **Prebiotic food vs. synbiotic framing**: Not an either/or. Seed says: "Ideally it's great to have both. A synbiotic can't replace the volume/variety of prebiotic foods from diet."

   **Source Quality Rules (MANDATORY — Peer-Reviewed Only):**
