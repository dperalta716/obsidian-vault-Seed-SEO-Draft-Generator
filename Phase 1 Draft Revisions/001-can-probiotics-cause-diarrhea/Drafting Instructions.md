- **Dosing advice**: "Starting with smaller doses" and "gradually increasing" are OK but should note this is a short-term approach and people "may miss out on the full spectrum of benefits of the clinically studied/supported dose"

## REVISION 3: Add Specific Dosing Ranges with CFU vs AFU (HIGH PRIORITY)

### What to Add
Specific dosing guidance WITH a distinction between CFU and AFU measurement methods.

### Where to Place
In the **"Do All Probiotics Cause Diarrhea?"** section, EXPAND the existing "Dosage (AFU/CFU Count)" bullet point.

### Content Guidelines
Expand the dosage bullet to approximately 100-125 words covering:

1. **General dosing ranges**:
   - Low doses: Less than 10 billion CFUs
   - Moderate/studied doses: 10-20 billion CFUs for adults
   - Children: typically 5-10 billion CFUs

2. **CFU vs AFU distinction** (CRITICAL for Seed differentiation):
   - CFU (Colony-Forming Units) is the traditional measurement
   - AFU (Active Fluorescent Units) is **a more precise measurement** that identifies truly viable, active bacteria
   - AFU counts only the bacteria that are actually alive and functional, not just those that can form colonies
   - This matters because you want to know how many ACTIVE bacteria you're actually getting

3. **Key message**: Match doses to what's been studied for specific strains; higher isn't automatically better

### Citation Requirement
- May need citation for dosing recommendations (AAFP guidelines recommend 10-20B for adults)
- The AFU vs CFU distinction may reference Seed's own methodology

### Tone Reminder
Keep it practical and empowering — help readers make informed choices. Avoid making it sound overly technical. Example: "Think of it this way: CFU tells you how many bacteria CAN grow in a lab, while AFU tells you how many are actually alive and ready to work in your gut."
