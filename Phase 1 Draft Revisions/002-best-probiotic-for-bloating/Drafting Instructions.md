## Seed SciCare POV Summary

### Key Seed Perspectives Found

- **Fermented foods vs. probiotics**: Seed distinguishes "Live Dietary Microbes (LDM)" from true probiotics. Fermented foods don't meet the scientific definition of probiotics (need defined dose, demonstrated benefit, live at consumption). Cannot casually recommend fermented foods as "probiotic alternatives."

- **Transient nature/colonization**: Probiotics don't colonize the gut permanently - they're transient and no longer recoverable in stool 1-4 weeks after stopping. Benefits come from specific interactions with resident microbes and local cells, not permanent residence or "repopulation."

- **Strain specificity**: Benefits are strain-specific and clinically assessed at the strain level. Data cannot be applied beyond the strain studied. Strain designation (e.g., "BR3" not just "_B. breve_") is critical.

- **Acclimation period**: Temporary acclimation is normal when starting probiotics, confined to GI system (changes in stool patterns, mild bloating/gas during first few days/weeks). This is NOT a "die-off" or "Herxheimer reaction" - those concepts are scientifically unsubstantiated for probiotics.

- **SIBO/IBS populations**: DS-01 has not been studied in SIBO populations specifically; cannot comment on efficacy in this condition. High-quality trials of probiotics in SIBO are limited, and strain- and dosage-specific protocols have not yet been defined. For IBS, some probiotic strains have been studied, but DS-01 has not been studied specifically for IBS.
