## Step 4: Revised Article Outline

### Proposed Structure

**H1**: What Are Probiotics? A Science-Based Guide

**Overview** (3 bullets):
- Probiotics are live microorganisms that, when taken in adequate amounts, provide a health benefit -- and not everything labeled "probiotic" meets that standard
- Research has linked specific probiotic strains to digestive, immune, and other health benefits, but effects depend on the strain, dose, and individual
- Choosing a probiotic backed by clinical evidence, verified potency, and survivability testing can help you find one that actually works

**Introduction** (~150 words):
- Open with the WHO-endorsed definition (featured snippet target)
- Address the core user question immediately
- Acknowledge the confusion in the marketplace
- Bridge to why the definition matters

**H2: How Does Science Define a Probiotic?** (~300 words):
- WHO/ISAPP definition breakdown
- Three criteria: live, adequate amounts, proven benefit
- Strain specificity explained (golden retriever vs. french bulldog analogy from POV)
- Link to [Microbiome 101](https://seed.com/cultured/microbiome-101/)

**H2: How Do Probiotics Work?** (~300 words):
- Transient nature (NOT colonizers -- use POV language)
- Four mechanisms: immune interactions, metabolite production, gut motility, gut barrier reinforcement
- Use POV-sourced language and references
- Links to [Gut Barrier Integrity](https://seed.com/cultured/why-is-gut-barrier-integrity-important/) and [Gut-Immune Connection](https://seed.com/cultured/the-gut-immune-connection/)
