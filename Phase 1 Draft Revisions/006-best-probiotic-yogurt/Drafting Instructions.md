### Content Requirements
Write approximately 150-200 words introducing a practical checklist. Include:

**Checklist items:**

1. **Look for "Live and Active Cultures" seal or statement**
   - This indicates at least 100 million CFU per gram at manufacture
   - Not a guarantee of probiotic status, but better than nothing

2. **Check for specific strain names**
   - Look for designations like *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG or *Bifidobacterium lactis* BB-12
   - Generic species names without strain identifiers (just "L. acidophilus") tell you less

3. **Watch the sugar content**
   - Aim for 6-8 grams or less per serving
   - Excess added sugar may work against microbial benefits
   - Plain is usually best â€” add your own fruit

4. **Verify it hasn't been heat-treated after fermentation**
   - Look for phrases like "heat-treated after culturing" â€” this kills live microbes
   - Products in the refrigerated section are generally safer bets

5. **Check the expiration date**
   - Live microbes degrade over time
   - Fresher = more likely to contain viable organisms

### Framing
Open with something like: "Since most yogurts won't meet true probiotic standards, here's how to maximize your LDM exposure and make smarter choices at the store."
