## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Seed's Differentiation Opportunities

1. **Strain specificity > drink format** — No competitor discusses which specific strains have clinical evidence. They treat all "probiotic drinks" as roughly equivalent. Seed can reframe: the vehicle (drink vs. capsule) matters less than the strains, dose, and whether they survive digestion.

2. **Survivability is the gap** — Only Verywell briefly mentions viability concerns. No competitor discusses acid resistance, delivery technology, or what happens to bacteria between the bottle and your colon. DS-01's ViaCap technology and SHIME testing directly address this.

3. **Synbiotic advantage** — No competitor discusses the prebiotic + probiotic combination. Seed's MAPP (pomegranate-derived prebiotics) is a genuine differentiator.

4. **Clinical evidence hierarchy** — Competitors cite generic probiotic studies. Seed can discuss finished-product clinical trials (Harvard IBS trial, Health Canada trial) as a higher standard of evidence.

5. **Sugar/dose trade-off** — Multiple competitors flag sugar as a problem in commercial drinks. Seed can align with this concern while positioning capsule-based synbiotics as avoiding the sugar issue entirely.

### Recommended Narrative Arc
1. Define probiotic drinks clearly (answer the search query immediately)
2. Walk through the main types (kefir, kombucha, yogurt drinks, probiotic sodas)
3. Explain what to actually look for (strain identity, dose, survivability — not just "live cultures")
4. Address the sugar concern that competitors have already raised
5. Introduce the concept that format matters less than formulation (Seed's angle)
6. Close with practical guidance on choosing evidence-based probiotics

### Dirk Gevers Quote Angle
Focus on strain specificity and survivability: "The question isn't really whether a drink contains probiotics — it's whether those specific strains have been studied, at that dose, and whether they're still alive when they reach your colon."

### DS-01 Mention Strategy (1-3 mentions)
1. When discussing capsule alternatives that avoid sugar concerns
2. When mentioning clinical testing and strain-specific evidence
3. Optional: when discussing synbiotic approach (prebiotic + probiotic)
