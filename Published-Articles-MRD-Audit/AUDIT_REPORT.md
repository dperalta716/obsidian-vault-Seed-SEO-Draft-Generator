# MRD Audit — 6 Published DS-01 Articles
Date: 2026-04-16
Audit basis: MRD-only policy per Kateri Corvetti directive (MRD supersedes SciCare POV, DS-01 PDP page, DS-01 Science Reference page)

---

## Executive Summary

The six articles were written under the prior multi-source regime (SciCare POV + PDP + Science Reference), which gave writers broad latitude to cite strain-level Latin names, individual ingredient studies, and editorial "standard advice vs. Seed POV" framings. The new MRD-only policy removes that latitude. The audit finds:

| # | Slug | Classification | Headline issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | signs-probiotics-are-working-guide | 🟡 MODERATE | Strain-level Latin IDs; non-MRD citations for skin, heart, folate, barrier; cardiovascular claim outside MRD |
| 2 | best-probiotics-for-weight-loss-guide | 🔴 CRITICAL | Entire topic (weight loss) sits outside MRD-approved claim territory. "Feeling lighter" paired with weight-loss framing is explicitly prohibited |
| 3 | best-probiotic-for-women-strain-benefits-guide | 🟠 MAJOR | Heavy strain-level naming (`L. crispatus` DSM32717/DSM32720/DSM32716, `L. plantarum` CECT 7527, etc.); many non-MRD citations; cardiovascular and bone-health claims outside MRD; vaginal-probiotic / VS-01-adjacent content is not DS-01 MRD territory |
| 4 | how-long-take-probiotics-duration-guide | 🟡 MODERATE | Dirk Gevers quote on transience is generally MRD-safe, but article cites unpublished Napier 2024 MO1898 abstract (now superseded by Napier 2025/2026 published papers); "non-fermenting prebiotic" language; strain-level IDs (LGG, BB-07, NCFM) |
| 5 | best-probiotic-for-men-strain-specific-guide | 🟠 MAJOR | Dense strain-level Latin (`L. plantarum` CECT 7527/7528/7529, KABP011/012/013, ECGC 13110402, `L. reuteri` CRL 1098, `B. adolescentis` BA5); cardiovascular/cholesterol claims; prostate/testosterone extrapolation outside MRD |
| 6 | do-probiotics-expire-shelf-life-storage-guide | 🟢 MINOR | Mostly product/packaging stability content that aligns with MRD's survivability pillar. Minor tweaks to strain mentions, DS-01 CTA language |

**High-level patterns:**

1. **Every article over-names strains at the strain-ID level** — MRD pins language at genus level (`Lactobacillus`, `Bifidobacterium`). This is the single most consistent violation.
2. **Every article except #6 cites studies the MRD Claims Library does not sanction** — Costabile 2017, Padro 2024, Drago 2011, Navarro-López 2021, Pompei 2007, Strozzi 2008 (MRD only uses Strozzi via Claims Library), Del Piano 2010 (MRD does allow via Claims Library), LeBlanc 2013, D'Aimmo 2023, and many others are from the now-retired Science Reference strain-study table.
3. **Cardiovascular/cholesterol is conspicuously absent from MRD** and appears in 4 of 6 articles. This needs a Kateri judgment call: retire or retain.
4. **No article uses the MRD disclaimer-symbol system** (°, ˆ, †, \*, \*\*, ††, Δ, µ). None reference Allegretti 2026, the n=350 trial, or the "72% never/rarely bloated" stat.
5. **No article cites a forbidden PDP marketing quote** ("75% reported improved regularity," "87% feel more in control," "92% have recommended it") — that's a clean result.
6. **No article uses old "Harvard / BIDMC / Beth Israel IBS" framing.** Article #4 does reference the Napier MO1898 2024 *abstract*, which is now superseded.
7. **No article credits SD-BR3-IT as a skin strain** — that specific risk did not materialize.
8. **Voice/framing is lightly SciCare-POV-flavored** ("Standard advice says X, but…" tonal move) in #2 and #3, but not heavy-handed. This is easier to edit than the citation problem.
9. **Quantitative DS-01 claims (22% barrier, 17x beneficial bacteria, 72% never/rarely bloated)** are *entirely absent* from these articles — which is interesting. The articles pre-date Allegretti 2026 publication, so they predate the opportunity to use those claims. Rewriting these articles is actually an opportunity to *add* MRD-sanctioned podium claims that aren't currently there.

### [1] signs-probiotics-are-working-guide

**Classification:** 🟡 MODERATE
**Word count:** ~1,800
**Top-level summary:** A 6-section "signs your probiotics are working" article covering digestion, immune, skin, gut barrier, gut-brain, oral health, plus cardiovascular and micronutrient synthesis as "silent benefits." Most of the claim territory *overlaps* with MRD (digestion, immune, skin via prebiotic, gut barrier, micronutrient synthesis), but the article substantiates those claims with non-MRD citations and uses strain-level Latin names MRD no longer permits.

#### Hard violations

1. **Strain-level Latin IDs (MRD = genus level only).**
   - "Certain probiotic strains, especially from the species *Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* and *Bifidobacterium lactis*, are particularly effective at supporting immune function."
   - "*Lactobacillus salivarius* LS01 and *Bifidobacterium lactis* have been studied for their role in supporting the gut-skin connection"
   - "*Lactiplantibacillus plantarum* KABP011, KABP012, and KABP013 can support heart health"
   - "*Bifidobacterium adolescentis* can support the synthesis of B vitamins"

2. **Non-MRD clinical citations.** The article's citation list uses studies *not* in the MRD Claims Library:
   - Drago 2011 (LS01 atopic dermatitis) — was in Science Reference, not MRD
   - Navarro-López 2021 (probiotics in dermatology) — retired source
   - La Fata 2018 (barrier) — retired
   - Di Vincenzo 2023 (permeability/inflammation) — retired
   - Padro 2024 (KABP strains cholesterol) — retired, **plus MRD has no cardiovascular claim**
   - D'Aimmo 2023 (folate bifidos) — retired (MRD uses Strozzi 2008 only for micronutrient)
   - LeBlanc 2013 (bacteria as vitamin suppliers) — retired
   - Latvala 2024, Iemoli 2012, Xiao 2006 — Iemoli and Xiao ARE in MRD for immune (can stay); Latvala is not
   - Olsen & Yamazaki 2019 (oral microbiome) — not in MRD; mouth/throat is not an MRD topic

3. **Cardiovascular claim outside MRD territory.** Explicit quote to flag:
   > "Certain probiotic strains (like *Lactiplantibacillus plantarum* KABP011, KABP012, and KABP013) can support heart health by helping to maintain blood cholesterol levels already in the normal range."

   MRD has no cardiovascular/cholesterol pillar. This paragraph needs to be cut or softened to generic "whole body health" language that MRD does permit under the Retention pillar.

4. **Mouth/throat microbiome as a "sign probiotics are working."** Not in MRD. Section #6 (Healthier Mouth and Throat) is entirely outside approved claim territory and should probably be cut.

5. **Missing disclaimer symbols throughout.** No claim in this article carries °, †, Δ, or µ. Every claim that reads like a benefit statement should carry °. Any quantitative DS-01-specific claim (not present here) would need its symbol.

6. **Timeline-of-Benefits mismatch.** The article's timeline ("first 2–4 weeks… 4–8 weeks… after 8 weeks") does not match MRD's Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 14 / Day 30 / Day 42+ Restore-Rebalance-Optimize framework. MRD bloating-impact claim is "in just 1 week," regularity is "in 2 weeks," 72% never/rarely bloated is at week 4. This article's framing ("initial digestive changes within 2-4 weeks") is inconsistent with the new trial data.

#### Softer signals

- Dirk Gevers is only mentioned as expert reviewer; the in-body expert quotes are Dr. Zain Kassam (CMO), which is fine, but several of his quoted claims ("microbes play a role in impacting the nervous system… even influence powerful molecules in the brain like oxytocin") are not MRD-substantiated and should be either cut or attributed purely as expert opinion without the implication of clinical backing.
- "Gut-brain axis" deep dive (Section 5) goes beyond MRD. MRD has no mood/mental-clarity/stress/sleep claim pillar for DS-01. This whole section is a candidate for trimming.

#### Estimated edit footprint
**30–40% rewrite.** Digestion + gut barrier + micronutrient sections can be edited in place with citation swaps (Allegretti 2026, Strozzi 2008, Del Piano 2010) and genus-level language. Cardiovascular, mouth/throat, and gut-brain sections need to be cut or collapsed into a much smaller "whole-body health" note under the MRD Retention pillar. Replace the timeline with MRD's Restore/Rebalance/Optimize framework and cite Allegretti 2026 for "in 1 week" bloating-impact + "72% never/rarely" at week 4.

#### Notes / edge cases
- Keep: immune health section (Iemoli 2012 and Xiao 2006 ARE in MRD).
- Keep: skin appearance via prebiotic (swap in Chakkalakal 2022 and specify "in 4 weeks" with the required disclaimer).
- Judgment call: whether to keep the "gut-brain" signal as a softer "some users report better wellbeing" framing or drop it entirely. MRD doesn't support it.

### [2] best-probiotics-for-weight-loss-guide

**Classification:** 🔴 CRITICAL
**Word count:** ~2,200
**Top-level summary:** A long-form article positioning probiotics as plausible weight-management adjuncts. Reviews SCFA appetite signaling, GLP-1, fat storage, gut barrier LPS, and names several strains (*L. gasseri* SBT2055, *L. rhamnosus* CGMCC1.3724, *B. animalis lactis* B420, *Akkermansia muciniphila* WST01). **Weight loss is not an MRD claim territory at all.** This article is structurally incompatible with MRD.

#### Hard violations

1. **Topic sits entirely outside MRD approved claim territory.** MRD's claim pillars are: bloating/gas, regularity/constipation, digestive QOL, gut barrier, immune, micronutrient synthesis, skin (prebiotic 4-week), survivability, gut microbiome. Weight loss is absent. The entire article premise — "can probiotics help with weight loss" — is outside the MRD substantiation envelope.

2. **"Helps you feel lighter" pairing problem.** MRD explicitly says:
   > *"Use of 'feeling lighter' should always be paired with 'more often' to ensure it is not interpreted as a weight loss claim… ensure it is paired with language on regularity, bloating, gas benefits to avoid interpretation as a weightloss claim."*

   This article's existence as a stand-alone piece on probiotics + weight management is exactly what MRD is trying to prevent. Even if the body never uses the word "lighter," the SEO target keyword ("best probiotics for weight loss") sits in forbidden territory.

3. **Strain-level Latin IDs (extensive):**
   - "*Lactobacillus gasseri* SBT2055"
   - "*Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* CGMCC1.3724"
   - "*Bifidobacterium animalis* subsp. *lactis* B420… *B. lactis* CECT 8145"
   - "*Akkermansia muciniphila* WST01 (pasteurized)"
   - "*Akkermansia muciniphila*… *Christensenellaceae*"
   - "*Lactobacillus acidophilus*" cited as potentially promoting weight gain

4. **Non-MRD citations throughout.** Kadooka 2010 (SBT2055), Sanchez 2014 (CGMCC1.3724), Stenman 2016 (B420), Pedret 2019 (CECT 8145), Zhang 2025 (Akkermansia), Goodrich 2014 (Christensenellaceae), Depommier 2019 (Akkermansia proof-of-concept), Turnbaugh 2006 (obesity microbiome), Cani 2008 (metabolic endotoxemia), Million 2012 (Lactobacillus species weight-gain meta) — **none** of these are in MRD's Claims Library.

5. **Dirk Gevers quote goes beyond MRD substantiation.** Quote:
   > "It's not just about adding bacteria—it's about what each strain is built to do and how that interacts with the unique ecosystem in your gut. The same strain might behave differently in two people, depending on what microbes are already there and what signals their bodies are sending."

   The quote itself is generic enough to survive, but it's positioned inside a weight-loss article, implying probiotics + weight management. That context is not MRD-supported.

6. **Mechanistic deep-dives beyond MRD's Timeline-of-Benefits:**
   - GLP-1 and PYY appetite hormones — not in MRD
   - Bile acid metabolism and fat cell signaling — not in MRD
   - LPS/metabolic endotoxemia pathway — not in MRD
   - Inflammation-obesity pathway — not in MRD

7. **Dr. Zain Kassam quote tied to weight-management context.** Contextually contaminated by topic, even though the literal content ("consistency is key; timing is not") is fine in isolation.

8. **Cross-linking to other problem articles.** Article links internally to "best-probiotic-for-women-strain-benefits-guide" and "best-probiotic-for-men-strain-specific-guide" — both of which are also flagged for heavy rewrites below, compounding the SEO internal-link problem.

#### Softer signals

- "Standard advice vs. Seed POV" editorial framing appears multiple times ("more probiotics = less belly fat" is like assuming any screwdriver works on every screw). Without SciCare POV as a source, these framings have no document backing them.

#### Estimated edit footprint
**80%+ rewrite or retire entirely.** There is no way to make a "best probiotics for weight loss" article MRD-compliant because weight loss is not an MRD claim. Three paths:

- **Retire:** 301 redirect to a related digestive QOL or bloating article.
- **Topic-reshape:** Rewrite as "probiotics, gut microbiome, and metabolic health" — but this is still outside MRD and likely not fixable.
- **Narrow radically:** Rewrite as "bloating that feels like weight" → MRD's "helps you feel lighter more often" messaging. This requires losing 80%+ of current content and the SEO keyword rationale is gone.

#### Notes / edge cases
- This is the clearest candidate for **pull + do not replace** in my view. Strongly recommend Kateri/Sydney adjudicate before any editing effort is spent.
- The SEO traffic value of "best probiotics for weight loss" is probably high. A business decision, not a compliance one.

### [4] how-long-take-probiotics-duration-guide

**Classification:** 🟡 MODERATE
**Word count:** ~2,200
**Top-level summary:** Timeline-focused article on how long to take probiotics for different goals (acute digestive upset, antibiotic recovery, chronic digestive issues, general gut maintenance). Includes a Dirk Gevers quote on transience, and a citation to a Napier 2024 *abstract* on DS-01 during/after antibiotics. Generally MRD-adjacent in framing — the transience angle is consistent with MRD's implicit "take it daily" positioning. Main issues are specific outdated citations and a handful of strain names.

#### Hard violations

1. **Napier MO1898 2024 abstract citation is now superseded.**
   > "[Napier et al, 2024] MO1898 Daily Supplementation with a Multi-species Synbiotic (DS-01) During and After Antibiotic Treatment Protects Against the Loss of Low-Abundance Bacterial Species While Enhancing Gut Barrier Integrity."

   Per the brief, Napier et al. published in *Nutrients* 2025 and *Antibiotics* 2026 should replace the 2024 abstract. Citation update required.

2. **Strain-level Latin IDs:**
   - "*Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus* GG is well-studied for antibiotic-associated diarrhea (AAD)"
   - "*Lactobacillus acidophilus* NCFM & *Bifidobacterium lactis* Bi-07" (in Ringel-Kulka 2011 citation #13)
   - "*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG" again in FAQ for pregnancy

3. **"Non-fermenting prebiotics (like punicalagins from pomegranate)."** MRD's current language is **"non-fiber polyphenol prebiotic"** — "non-fermenting" is the old framing. Update needed.

4. **Non-MRD citations.** Most of the 29 citations in this article are about timeline/transience/RCT design and are generic academic sources rather than substantiating specific DS-01 claims — so they're less problematic than Articles 2/3/5 — but the ones tied to specific strain-level claims (Ringel-Kulka 2011, Szajewska 2019 for LGG gastroenteritis, Mazziotta 2023 — MRD-approved for immune but cited here in "behind-the-scenes effects," Gomes 2024 for LGG in pregnancy) should be reviewed against MRD.

5. **Dirk Gevers quote is MRD-compatible but needs verification.**
   > "Contrary to popular belief, science supports that most probiotics are transient. There's little evidence that probiotics 'colonize' long-term or that colonization is required for beneficial outcomes. Probiotics travel through the colon, impact resident microbes, and get expelled through stool. This is why their benefits are generally confined to the time they're taken."

   This is *implicit* in MRD's "take it daily" framing but is not explicitly stated as a claim in the Claims Library. Not a violation per se, but worth Kateri's eye — specifically the phrase "impact resident microbes" which is a softer version of MRD-sanctioned "increases beneficial bacteria by 17xΔ" and "increases beneficial bacteria [in the gut microbiome]°". If we're going to make a resident-microbe claim, MRD wants it phrased with the approved locked string.

6. **Missing opportunity to use MRD-approved Timeline-of-Benefits.** The article's timelines (week 1 for acute, 2–6 weeks for antibiotics, 4–12 weeks for chronic, 3+ months for general) don't line up with MRD's Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 14 / Day 30 / Day 42+ framework. Under MRD-only policy, the framing should align with the Allegretti 2026 trial timeline (bloating impact at 1 week, regularity strains at 2 weeks, 72% never/rarely bloated at 4 weeks, constipation relief at 6 weeks).

#### Softer signals

- Bone-health / hormone-health content absent — good.
- Cardiovascular content absent — good.
- Weight-loss framing absent — good.
- The "probiotics are transient" angle is actually one of the stronger MRD-compatible narratives in any of these 6 articles — this is the closest any article comes to MRD-safe territory out of the gate.

#### Estimated edit footprint
**15–25% rewrite.** Biggest lift: swap the Napier 2024 abstract to Napier 2025 *Nutrients* and Napier 2026 *Antibiotics*; update "non-fermenting" to "non-fiber polyphenol"; genus-level the strain callouts; restructure the timeline block around MRD's Restore/Rebalance/Optimize phases and cite Allegretti 2026.

#### Notes / edge cases
- This is the cleanest "edit in place" article of the six.
- Worth using as the template / precedent for how to bring the other articles into MRD compliance.

### [6] do-probiotics-expire-shelf-life-storage-guide

**Classification:** 🟢 MINOR
**Word count:** ~2,000
**Top-level summary:** Stability/packaging/storage-focused article with extensive coverage of CFU/AFU viability, capsule technology, and DS-01 ViaCap® mentions. Most content aligns with MRD's "Engineered to Survive" pillar. Few claim violations; mostly cosmetic edits needed.

#### Hard violations

1. **Strain-level Latin IDs (light):**
   - "*Bifidobacterium animalis* subsp. *lactis* and *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG… are resilient to various forms of environmental and manufacturing stress"
   - "*Lactobacillus acidophilus* and *Bifidobacterium animalis* subsp. *lactis* do not have the ability to break down the proteins in milk"

   These are narrowly descriptive (survivability) rather than benefit-claim-attached, so the severity is lower — but MRD's standard is genus-level and these should still be softened.

2. **DS-01 / ViaCap® callouts do not carry required symbols:**
