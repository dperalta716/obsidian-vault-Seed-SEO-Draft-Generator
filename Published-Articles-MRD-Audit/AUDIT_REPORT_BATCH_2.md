### Cross-batch comparison

Batch 1 had one 🟢, two 🟡, two 🟠, and one 🔴 (Weight Loss) spread across informational and converter pages. Batch 2 is weighted heavier — zero 🟢, one 🟡 (Antibiotics), four 🟠. The reason is simple: **these articles were specifically written to convert**, which means they lean harder on strain-specific authority signals and detailed recommendation blocks — exactly the surface area that MRD has pulled back from. The article that looked easiest (Antibiotics, because the Napier 2025/2026 publications actually exist to substantiate the topic) still has a strain-level recommendation engine in the "Best Strains" section that can't survive genus-level rewrite without flattening the conversion lever.

No article cites the forbidden PDP stats (75% / 87% / 92%) or the retired "Harvard / BIDMC" framing. Clean on those. None credit SD-BR3-IT as a skin strain. Clean. None use "non-fermenting prebiotic" language in this batch (batch 1's Article 4 was the outlier). The Allegretti 2026 data, 22% gut barrier, 17x *Lactobacillus*, 72% never/rarely bloated, and the "in just 1 week" bloating-impact claim are **absent from all five articles** — all five predate Allegretti publication, which means rewrites are an *opportunity* to add MRD-sanctioned podium claims these articles currently lack.

### Top-line recommendations (compliance risk vs conversion risk)

1. **Antibiotics** — edit in place. Napier 2025 + Napier 2026 *strengthen* the article; genus-level the strain recommendations and anchor to DS-01-specific substantiation.
2. **SIBO** — needs Kateri judgment. SIBO is a diagnosed clinical condition MRD avoids on purpose. Either neuter efficacy stats + reposition as "probiotics and SIBO-like discomfort," retire/redirect to bloating, or keep as pure-educational without DS-01 CTA.
3. **Teenagers** — needs business decision. DS-01 isn't formulated for adolescents; the article is selling an adult product to a teen audience. Retire unless pediatric SEO value is high or a pediatric Seed product is coming.
4. **Women / Men** — unchanged from Batch 1: gender framing has no MRD support; cardiovascular/bone/prostate sit outside MRD. Needs editorial + business adjudication before edits start.

### [2] best-probiotic-for-antibiotics-guide

**Classification:** 🟡 MODERATE
**Word count:** ~2,100 (8 min read / 28 citations)
**Edit % estimate:** 25–35% rewrite
**Top-line summary:** Covers antibiotic-microbiome disruption, mechanisms probiotics use during antibiotic treatment, strain-specific recommendations for antibiotic-associated diarrhea (AAD), timing guidance (2-hour spacing), prebiotic support, fermented-food caveat, and safety contraindications. **Topic sits inside MRD-adjacent territory via Napier 2025 (*Nutrients*) and Napier 2026 (*Antibiotics*)**, which MRD-compliant messaging can lean on — but the article as published predates those papers and uses only strain-level literature.

#### Hard violations

1. **Strain-level Latin IDs — structurally load-bearing:**
   - "*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG (LGG)" — full subsection on AAD in adults and children
   - "*Saccharomyces boulardii*" — full subsection as "friendly yeast" for AAD + C. diff
   - "Specific *Bifidobacterium* Strains": "*B. longum* BB536… for maintaining gut regularity"
   - FAQs: "*Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG and *Saccharomyces boulardii* are two of the most studied microbes for antibiotic-associated diarrhea"
   - Editorial bias: "Full Strain Identification: Genus, species, and strain ID. If a label just says 'Lactobacillus,' that's like saying 'dog'…" — pro-strain-naming stance conflicts with MRD's genus-level policy.

2. **Non-MRD citations** (mostly generic mechanism/AAD literature): Goodman 2021, Cai 2017, Chung 2025, Neut 2017, Ehrhardt 2016, Pozzoni 2012, Wong/Odamaki 2019, Szajewska 2015. One interesting inclusion: **Tierney 2023** (*AEM*) is the DS-01 SHIME paper with Gevers as co-author — not in MRD Claims Library but IS a DS-01 study. Kateri call on whether ancillary DS-01 papers are citable.

3. **Missed opportunity: Napier 2025 *Nutrients* + Napier 2026 *Antibiotics*** — per the brief's list, these are the DS-01-specific antibiotic-recovery trials that should be the spine of this article. **Not cited.**

4. **No MRD podium-claim integration** — no 22% gut barrier†, no 17x beneficial bacteriaΔ, no Allegretti 2026, no "ViaCap® safeguards probiotics [from stomach acid] through digestion" (natural fit in the stability section), no disclaimer symbols.

5. **Topic-breadth check:** antibiotic-recovery IS inside MRD via Napier. Topic is clean; execution is out of date.

6. **Dirk Gevers quote** on fermented foods is MRD-consistent ("wildcard… can't know which microbes are present… targeted delivery and measurable impact") — maps to MRD's "engineered to survive" positioning. Keep.

#### Softer signals
- "Skilled visitors, not microbial superheroes" — lifestyle-voice framing for transience. Consistent with MRD implicit "take it daily" positioning.
- "Treat it like a neighborhood under renovation" analogy. Survivable.
- Editorial bias toward strain-level-naming-as-quality-signal. Needs to be neutralized to "genus + species + well-characterized in clinical trials on the finished product."

#### Conversion levers at risk

The conversion engine is the strain-recommendation block (LGG, *S. boulardii*, BB536, multi-strain). A reader searching "best probiotic for antibiotics" is comparison-shopping, and strain names function as authority anchors.

**Strong compliant rewrite path:** keep structure (how antibiotics affect gut → what probiotics do → which to choose → how to take → beyond probiotics → FAQs); replace the strain subsections with a single "What DS-01® offers during and after antibiotics" block anchored to Napier 2025 and Napier 2026; keep 2-hour timing guidance; add "Engineered to survive" + "ViaCap® safeguards probiotics [from stomach acid] through digestion"; keep prebiotic/fiber and safety sections. The Napier additions *strengthen* the conversion case because they're DS-01-specific substantiation for exactly the user's query.

**Conversion risk: LOW-MODERATE.** The rewrite upgrades authority (two peer-reviewed DS-01-specific trials vs. generic strain literature). Only downside is loss of LGG / *S. boulardii* brand-recognition anchor.

**Edit footprint:** 25–35% rewrite. **This is the cleanest rewrite opportunity in the batch — recommend editing first.**

**Edge cases:** (a) competitor-strain naming in explicit contrast ("LGG and *S. boulardii* are well-known, but DS-01® uses a different, clinically trialed consortium") — Kateri call; (b) 28 citations likely halve under MRD-only — check against draft-review workflow's 12-15 target.

### [4] probiotics-sibo-treatment-research-guide

**Classification:** 🟠 MAJOR
**Word count:** ~3,100 (9 min read / 19 citations)
**Edit % estimate:** 50–60% rewrite, or retire
**Top-line summary:** Deep-dive on probiotics for Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO). Covers SIBO mechanisms (hydrogen/methane/H2S), probiotic mechanisms (competitive exclusion, antimicrobial production, immune modulation), clinical meta-analysis results, methane-backfire warning, strain-specific recommendations, synbiotic evidence, timing, and comprehensive-approach framing. The article is substantively *good* research journalism — but SIBO is a clinical diagnosis, and MRD does not underwrite SIBO claims.

#### Hard violations

1. **Topic sits on a clinical-diagnosis boundary.** SIBO is a formally diagnosed condition (ICD-10: K63.821) confirmed by breath tests. None of MRD's claim pillars map to "treats SIBO" or "supports SIBO management" — making probiotic recommendations for a diagnosed clinical condition is a higher-risk regulatory category than MRD supports.

2. **Non-DS-01 efficacy stats read as drug-like claims:**
   > "62.8% of people had negative breath tests after probiotic use"
   > "Hydrogen levels dropped by 36.35 ppm"
   > "93.3% tested negative for SIBO after 6 months"
   > "100% had no abdominal pain"

   From Zhong 2017 meta-analysis and Khalighi 2015 pilot — **not DS-01 data.** Presented adjacent to DS-01 positioning, these read as implying DS-01 produces those outcomes. MRD's Copy Compliance Guidance on Allegretti requires further translations be reviewed — these are unapproved further translations.

3. **Strain-level Latin IDs, many never in DS-01:** *S. boulardii* (yeast), *B. clausii* and *B. coagulans* (spore-formers, named with strain codes "MTCC 5856 + Unique IS2"), *L. acidophilus* / *L. casei* / *L. plantarum*. These are competitor-landscape strains in a research-review, not DS-01 strains. Strict MRD reading: out. Loose reading: research-review context survives. Kateri call — but the article concludes with an implicit DS-01 CTA, pushing toward the strict read.

4. **Methane-SIBO backfire warning is a direct contraindication for DS-01** ("people who had taken probiotics were more likely to test positive for methane-producing SIBO"; methane-SIBO listed under "When Probiotics Might Not Be Right for You"). Editorially honest, MRD-compatible in spirit, but structurally self-defeating as a conversion asset.

5. **Non-MRD citations** — all 19 are SIBO/probiotic literature, none in MRD Claims Library.

6. **MRD podium claims + DS-01 framing absent entirely.** DS-01 appears only via internal links, never in body copy. No 22%, no 17x, no 72%, no Allegretti.

7. **No Dirk Gevers quote** (reviewer is Sadie Barr). Brand-voice authority lever is thin.

#### Softer signals
- "Probiotic Paradox" framing, "quiet residential areas / downtown hub" analogy, "velvet rope bouncers" analogy — lifestyle voice, survivable.
- Decision tree framing ("start low and go slow," "track what you take") is MRD-neutral.
- Monochrome disclaimer style ("talk to your doctor," "probiotics are not a cure for SIBO") is regulatorily useful and should stay.

#### Conversion levers at risk

The authority signals here are (a) the 62.8% / 93.3% / 100% efficacy stats — **cannot survive MRD** since they're not DS-01 data; (b) strain-specific SIBO recommendations built around *S. boulardii*, *B. clausii*, *B. coagulans* — **none in DS-01**, so zero conversion overlap with the recommended product; (c) "comprehensive approach" framing — survives MRD.

**The article's recommendation engine is not DS-01-aligned** — it's recommending strains DS-01 doesn't contain. Either it's educational without conversion, or conversion without strain coverage. A compliant rewrite strips the efficacy percentages and repositions DS-01 as digestive-comfort support for readers whose SIBO-like symptoms (bloating, gas, regularity) map to MRD's bloating-impact and regularity claims — telling diagnosed-SIBO readers to work with a provider. Loses the specificity that ranked the article; gains compliance.

**Conversion risk: HIGH.** Current recommendation architecture is built around non-DS-01 strains.

#### Estimated edit footprint
**50–60% rewrite**, or retire.

#### Notes / edge cases
- **Major editorial question for Kateri/Sydney:** does Seed want to own "probiotics for SIBO" SEO territory at all? The topic is a clinical diagnosis, and DS-01 strain selection wasn't made with SIBO in mind. From a "compliance + conversion alignment" view, this is the single least-aligned article in the batch.
- **The methane-SIBO paragraph is important to preserve** — removing it would be worse (readers with methane-SIBO actively get worse with some probiotics; silence on this is harmful to readers and potentially to the brand).
- **"In vitro" evidence carve-out:** article cites a synbiotic in-vitro study (Tierney 2023 would be a natural swap target since that's the DS-01 SHIME paper). Could Tierney 2023 be added? It's not in the MRD Claims Library but IS a DS-01 paper. Kateri call.

## Recommendations

### Articles safe to edit in place (lowest conversion risk)

- **Antibiotics (🟡 MODERATE, 25–35% edit).** Clear path: swap strain-subsections to genus + DS-01 block; cite Napier 2025 and Napier 2026 as the DS-01-specific antibiotic-recovery substantiation; add MRD podium claims ("engineered to survive," "ViaCap® safeguards probiotics [from stomach acid]"), add Allegretti 2026 as secondary authority; preserve the 2-hour timing guidance and the prebiotic/fiber section. **This is the first article Kate should touch** — it's the cleanest rewrite opportunity and the Napier additions genuinely strengthen the article.

### Articles where edits will likely weaken conversion (Kate/Kateri judgment required)

- **Women (🟠 MAJOR, 60–70% edit).** Compliance risk of not updating is high (vaginal + cardiovascular + bone). Conversion risk of rewriting is also high (life-stage + whole-body pitch dissolves).
