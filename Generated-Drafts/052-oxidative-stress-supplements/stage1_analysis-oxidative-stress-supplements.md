## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Baseline Summary:

Competitive articles range from short consumer-focused explainers (~800-1200 words) to comprehensive academic reviews (15,000+ words). Most successful consumer articles are 1500-2500 words, include tables of supplements with benefits, explain the free radical/antioxidant balance clearly, and address both supplement and dietary approaches.

Medical authority sites (Cleveland Clinic, Healthline) emphasize food over supplements while supplement brand sites push specific products with citations to support claims. The sweet spot for a competitive article appears to be 1800-2200 words with a balanced approach that acknowledges both dietary and supplemental strategies.

All articles begin by explaining what oxidative stress is before diving into solutions. The most cited study is Pizzino 2017, which appears across multiple sources. Strong articles use 8-15 academic citations strategically placed to support key claims.

### Gaps & Opportunities for Seed:

- **Gap 1**: No article discusses bioavailability differences between supplement forms (opportunity for Seed's precision formulation angle)
- **Gap 2**: Limited discussion of gut-microbiome connection to oxidative stress (major Seed differentiator)
- **Gap 3**: Most articles don't address optimal dosing or timing of antioxidant supplements
- **Opportunity**: Contrast "antioxidant overload" thinking with targeted, precision approaches
- **Opportunity**: Sleep and oxidative stress connection mentioned but not deeply explored
- **Opportunity**: No discussion of circadian rhythm impact on oxidative stress/antioxidant function

### Recommended Approach:

1. **Meet baseline** by covering: Definition of oxidative stress, free radicals vs antioxidants, health effects/diseases, causes, key antioxidant supplements, dietary sources, mechanism of action
2. **Layer Seed perspective** via: Bioavailability of supplement forms, microbiome connection to oxidative stress, precision dosing philosophy, circadian/sleep connections
3. **Target word count**: 1800-2000 words (baseline articles range: 800-2500 words)
4. **Citation strategy**: Based on competitive patterns - aim for ~12-15 academic sources, prioritizing Pizzino 2017, Hajam 2022, and CoQ10/specific ingredient studies
