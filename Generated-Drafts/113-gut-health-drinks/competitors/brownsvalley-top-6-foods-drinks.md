## What Are Probiotics and Prebiotics?

**Probiotics** are live beneficial bacteria that support the balance of your gut microbiome. They occur naturally in your body and are essential for digestion.

Probiotics offer many scientifically proven benefits1. They support your health by:

- Helping fight off pathogens
- Strengthening the stomach lining
- Improving nutrient absorption
- Lowering inflammation
- Producing short-chain fatty acids that nourish gut cells

These advantages contribute to a happier, healthier gut and better wellness overall.

**Prebiotics** are also incredibly beneficial for digestive health, but they have a different function than probiotics.

### Probiotics vs. Prebiotics

Although probiotics and prebiotics work together to deliver the same benefits, they are responsible for separate roles.

The difference between probiotics and prebiotics:

- **Probiotics:** the beneficial bacteria that keep our body healthy by supporting digestion and strengthening the stomach lining
- **Prebiotics:** foods or food ingredients that naturally stimulate the growth or activity of probiotics

## Fermented Foods for Gut Health

Many fermented foods like kimchi, sauerkraut, and yogurt are rich in probiotics that help your gut microbiome flourish. Below are some of the delicious gut-friendly foods we carry at Browns Valley Market.

### Yogurt

Yogurt is made from fermenting milk with live bacteria. This process creates lactic acid, which gives yogurt its tangy flavor and creamy texture. It's also rich in gut-friendly probiotics!

You can add yogurt to smoothies, and savory dips, or layer it with your favorite seasonal fruits and granola for a delicious breakfast.

### Kimchi

Kimchi is a popular Korean side dish made from fermented vegetables like cabbage, radishes, and scallions. It's often seasoned with chili powder, garlic, and ginger to give it a mouthwatering, spicy kick. It's also incredibly rich in probiotics that help balance your gut's microbiome!

Kimchi can be enjoyed all on its own, added to soups and stews, or used as a flavorful topping for rice bowls and sandwiches.

### Sauerkraut

Sauerkraut is a German dish made from fermented shredded cabbage. Celebrated for its tangy and slightly acidic flavor, it's packed with probiotics that aid digestion and improve immunity1.

Sauerkraut can be enjoyed on its own, added to sandwiches and hot dogs, or used as a topping for soups and stews.
