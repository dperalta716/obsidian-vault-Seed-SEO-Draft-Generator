## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Baseline Summary:

Competitive articles range from brief nail care guides (~700 words) to comprehensive nutrient breakdowns (~2000 words). The most successful articles (Healthline, GoodRx) use a structured nutrient-by-nutrient format with specific dosages, food sources, and inline academic citations. All articles emphasize a food-first approach and caution that supplements only help if you're deficient.

The standard structure includes: introduction addressing user intent, individual sections per key nutrient (biotin featured prominently), deficiency symptoms, food sources for each nutrient, and a conclusion recommending dietary changes before supplementation. Medical authority (Mayo Clinic) and practitioner perspectives (dermatology) add credibility.

Word count range: 700-2000 words, with most comprehensive guides at 1500-2000 words. The sweet spot appears to be 1500-1800 words with ~8-15 academic citations.

### Gaps & Opportunities for Seed:

- **Gap 1**: No article discusses the microbiome connection to nutrient absorption for nail health - Seed can uniquely address this
- **Gap 2**: Limited discussion of bioavailable forms of supplements vs. standard forms - opportunity to highlight quality differences
- **Gap 3**: Collagen coverage is growing but evidence is still presented as "developing" - opportunity to provide more nuanced take
- **Gap 4**: Few articles discuss timeline expectations for seeing results from supplements
- **Gap 5**: Omega-3s are underrepresented despite evidence for skin barrier and inflammation benefits
- **Gap 6**: Magnesium is only covered in 1-2 articles despite its role in protein synthesis
- **Gap 7**: No article addresses the quality/purity concerns with nail supplements specifically

### Recommended Approach:

1. **Meet baseline** by covering: Biotin (primary), Iron, B vitamins (B12, folate), Zinc, Collagen, Protein/keratin, Vitamin C, food sources, diet vs supplements messaging
2. **Layer Seed perspective** via:
   - Microbiome connection to nutrient absorption (unique to Seed)
   - Bioavailable forms and precision dosing philosophy
   - Quality and purity considerations in supplement selection
   - DM-02 Daily Multivitamin positioning if ingredient alignment exists
3. **Target word count**: 1600-1800 words (baseline articles range: 700-2000)
4. **Citation strategy**: Aim for ~12-15 academic sources; leverage primary sources already identified (Hochman 1993, Chessa 2019, Vleminckx 2024) plus Seed claims documents
