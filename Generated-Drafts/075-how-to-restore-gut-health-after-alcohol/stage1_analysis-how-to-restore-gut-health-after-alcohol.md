# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: How to Restore Gut Health After Alcohol

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary intent**: Actionable recovery guidance — users want specific steps to repair gut damage after drinking alcohol (ranging from a rough weekend to chronic use).

**Core questions users are trying to answer**:
1. What does alcohol actually do to my gut?
2. How can I fix it — what should I eat, drink, or take?
3. How long will recovery take?
4. Can probiotics help after drinking?

**Secondary intents**:
- Understanding whether their symptoms (bloating, nausea, irregular bowel movements) are gut-related
- Reassurance that damage is reversible
- Specific foods/supplements that speed recovery

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Standard Advice to Challenge
1. **"Eat probiotic-rich foods like yogurt and kimchi"** — Standard in all 5 articles. Seed angle: Do the bacteria in fermented foods actually survive digestion? This is where clinically validated, precision-dosed probiotics differ.
2. **"Drink bone broth to heal the gut lining"** — Popular but lacks clinical trial evidence. Seed angle: Gut barrier restoration requires more than collagen; it requires rebalancing the microbial ecosystem.
3. **"Give it time and your gut will heal itself"** — Vague timeline advice. Seed angle: Research shows dysbiosis can persist 3+ weeks after abstinence; active microbiome support may accelerate recovery.

### Seed's Differentiation Opportunities
1. **Clinical validation**: No competitor cites a clinical trial for a specific probiotic. DS-01's Allegretti trial (n=350) is a massive differentiator.
2. **Survivability angle**: "Engineered to Survive" pillar is directly relevant — if fermented foods are the standard advice, the natural question is whether those bacteria survive digestion.
3. **Precision over generality**: While competitors say "eat probiotics," Seed can explain why dose, strain selection, and delivery mechanism matter for gut restoration.
4. **Gut barrier specificity**: DS-01's 22% gut barrier strengthening claim†° directly addresses alcohol's primary damage mechanism (intestinal permeability).
5. **Recovery timeline with evidence**: Can pair the 3-week dysbiosis data with DS-01's phased benefit timeline (Restore/Rebalance/Optimize) for a more specific recovery narrative.

### Recommended Article Structure
- H1: Gut-restoration-focused, benefit-oriented
- Intro: Relatable hook (morning-after scenario), direct answer
- H2: What alcohol actually does to your gut (mechanisms)
- H2: How your gut recovers (timeline, what helps)
- H2: Foods and habits that support gut restoration
- H2: Why a precision probiotic matters (DS-01 angle)
- The Key Insight: Warm, grounding close
- FAQs: 4 PAA questions above

### Citation target: 12-15 academic sources
