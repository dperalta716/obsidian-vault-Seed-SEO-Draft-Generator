## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Baseline Summary:

Top-ranking articles consistently clarify that ashwagandha is NOT a sedative but works indirectly through stress reduction and cortisol regulation over 2-12 weeks. Most articles cite the same core studies (Langade 2021, Lopresti 2021) and recommend 300-600mg doses. Safety information about liver injury risk is increasingly prominent.

The NIH article sets the gold standard for depth and citations (46+ references), while commercial health sites provide more accessible, consumer-friendly coverage with practical comparisons to melatonin. Word counts range widely from 800 to 3500 words, with the median around 1500 words.

All articles take care to distinguish ashwagandha from melatonin and other direct sleep aids, emphasizing that it works through stress reduction rather than sedation. The "adaptogen" concept is central to most explanations.

### Gaps & Opportunities for Seed:

- **Gap 1**: GABA receptor mechanism is mentioned but not deeply explained - opportunity for Seed's scientific depth
- **Gap 2**: Circadian rhythm connection is underexplored - Seed's PM-02 positioning could differentiate here
- **Gap 3**: Most articles focus on root extracts only - leaf chemistry differences rarely discussed
- **Gap 4**: Timeline expectations (2-12 weeks) could be better emphasized vs instant gratification culture
- **Gap 5**: Few articles address the 'best time to take' question comprehensively
- **Gap 6**: Quality/standardization of supplements (withanolide content) is mentioned but not deeply explored

### Recommended Approach:

1. **Meet baseline** by covering: Mechanism of action (cortisol, GABA, stress pathway), sleep research evidence, side effects/safety, dosage (300-600mg), adaptogen definition, comparison with melatonin, who should avoid
2. **Layer Seed perspective** via: GABA receptor science, circadian rhythm connection from PM-02 messaging, precision dosing philosophy, quality/standardization emphasis
3. **Target word count**: 1600-1800 words (baseline articles range: 800-3500, median ~1500)
4. **Citation strategy**: Aim for ~12-15 academic sources; prioritize Langade 2021, Cheah 2021, Lopresti 2021 as foundational; add Seed claims document sources for differentiation
