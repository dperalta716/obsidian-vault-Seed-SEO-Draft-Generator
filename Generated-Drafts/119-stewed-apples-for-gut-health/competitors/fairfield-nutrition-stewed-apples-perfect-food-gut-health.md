# Stewed Apples – The perfect food for gut health?

- **Source**: fairfieldnutrition.com.au
- **Author**: Stephen Ward (MSc)
- **URL**: https://fairfieldnutrition.com.au/2018/11/06/stewed-apple-recipe-for-a-healthy-gut/
- **Scraped**: 2026-06-19

---

Stewed apples positioned as a simple, powerful way to reduce gut inflammation, with claims for IBS, IBD, bloating, pain, and constipation.

## Topics covered
- Polyphenols (deep) — 5-10% absorbed in small intestine, rest fermented in colon; enhance beneficial bacteria, inhibit pathogens
- Soluble fibre / prebiotic (deep) — Bifidobacteria + Lactobacillus increase with 2 apples/day; SCFAs regulate leaky gut
- FODMAP sensitivity (medium) — caution for low-FODMAP dieters; pears as alternative
- Immune tolerance / food sensitivities (medium) — apples increase immune system tolerance
- Why cooking matters (medium) — cooking releases extra pectin; anti-inflammatory in animal studies
- Skins (light) — more polyphenols, fibre, minerals
- Recipe + cinnamon + adding probiotic powder (light)

## Claims & sources (external links)
- SCFAs shown to regulate leaky gut: http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.neulet.2016.02.009 (PRIMARY - DOI)
- PubMed 20024905: http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20024905 (PRIMARY)
- Pectin anti-inflammatory in animals: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20138982 (PRIMARY)

## Notable angles
- Frames inflammation as "the heart of all gut issues"
- Recommends sprinkling probiotic capsule on cooked apples
- Pears as alternative for apple-reactive people
