# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: Best Vegetables for Gut Health

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary Intent:** Informational -- users want a curated, trustworthy list of specific vegetables that support gut health, with clear reasoning for each recommendation.

**Core Questions Users Are Trying to Answer:**
- Which specific vegetables should I eat more of to improve my gut health?
- What makes certain vegetables better for the gut than others (fiber, prebiotics, polyphenols)?
- Are there vegetables I should avoid if I have digestive issues?
- How do vegetables support the gut microbiome specifically?

**Secondary Intent:** Some users may be dealing with digestive discomfort (bloating, irregularity) and looking for dietary solutions.

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Seed's Unique Angle (from SciCare POV Brief):
**Core narrative:** Alliums (especially garlic), cruciferous vegetables, and leafy greens are the most evidence-backed vegetables for gut health -- but variety matters more than volume. Garlic stands out as a prebiotic powerhouse with dual fiber + bioactive (allicin) benefits, though those with IBS should approach cautiously due to FODMAP content.

**Key differentiator vs. competitors:** None of the top 5 articles discuss:
- Fiber-specific fermentation patterns (Blecksmith 2025 -- different vegetables produce different SCFAs)
- Garlic's polysaccharide effect on tight junctions and gut barrier (Li 2026)
- Allicin as a distinct bioactive mechanism beyond fiber (Deng 2025)
- FODMAP tension with the best gut-health vegetables (garlic/onions)

### Standard Advice to Challenge:
1. **"Just eat more fiber"** -- Challenge with: variety of fiber types matters more than total fiber intake (Blecksmith 2025)
2. **"Fermented veggies are probiotic"** -- Clarify distinction per POV: fermented foods may contain live microorganisms but don't necessarily meet probiotic criteria
3. **"All vegetables are equally good"** -- Challenge with: specific vegetable families have distinct prebiotic and bioactive profiles

### DS-01 Angle (Topic Appropriateness Gate: PASSED):
- This is a food/ingredient topic -- DS-01 can be mentioned with objective differentiation framing
- Relevant claims: "increases beneficial bacteria in the gut microbiome°", "supports gut barrier integrity°", "formulated to target root causes of digestive distress°"
- "Engineered to Survive" pillar is relevant since the article discusses whether microbes from food survive digestion
- Probiotics should be discussed broadly first; DS-01 introduced separately as one example
