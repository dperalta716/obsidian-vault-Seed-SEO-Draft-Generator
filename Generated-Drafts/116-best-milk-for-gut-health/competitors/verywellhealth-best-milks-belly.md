# Discover the Top 9 Milks for a Happy Gut

By [Barbara Bolen, PhD](https://www.verywellhealth.com/dr-barbara-bolen-1944630)

Updated on April 18, 2026

Medically reviewed by
[Robert Burakoff, MD](https://www.verywellhealth.com/robert-burakoff-md-4777066)

### Key Takeaways

- Lactose-free milk helps people with lactose intolerance avoid stomach problems.
- Almond milk is a healthy option for people with IBS or milk allergies.
- Hemp milk has nutrients and is a good choice for vegetarians with lactose intolerance.


Choosing the right milk can ease digestion issues for those with IBS or lactose intolerance. Explore the best options like lactose-free, almond, or hemp milk to support gut health while meeting nutritional needs.

1

## Lactose-Free Milk

FreshSplash / Getty Images

People with lactose intolerance lack enough of the enzyme lactase to digest lactose easily. [**Lactose-free milk**](https://www.verywellhealth.com/lactose-free-milk-side-effects-11778243) is usually cow's milk with the lactose removed, letting those with lactose intolerance enjoy milk without digestive issues.

Lactose-free milk is a [**low-FODMAP choice**](https://www.verywellhealth.com/foods-on-the-low-fodmap-diet-1944679), , meaning it won't typically cause cramping or gas for those with [IBS or lactose intolerance](https://www.verywellhealth.com/ibs-vs-lactose-intolerance-11735820).

Cow's milk provides important nutrients like protein, vitamins, and calcium, which support [bone health](https://www.verywellhealth.com/stages-of-osteoporosis-5202548).

Other health experts question whether humans should be drinking milk at all. Some research does not support the claim that milk reduces fracture risk.1

Drinking milk could also bring about other health risks. Dairy milk has been associated with acne and eczema.2 A few studies have linked cow's milk to a higher risk of prostate, breast, and ovarian cancers.34

2

## Soy Milk

Natalia Gdovskaia / Getty Images

[**Soy milk**](https://www.verywellhealth.com/soy-milk-11897999) is made by soaking and grinding soybeans, then boiling and filtering the mixture to remove solids.

Nutritionally, soy milk is similar to cow's milk, offering 8 grams of protein per 8-ounce serving. It's lower in carbs and generally contains comparable levels of calcium and vitamin D.5

Soy milk made from soy protein extract is low in FODMAPs. If you have IBS, choose this type over soy milk made directly from soybeans, which contain the carbohydrate galacto-oligosaccharide (GOS).6

If you have IBS, make sure the soy milk you choose is made from soy protein extract by checking the label.

3

## Almond Milk

fcafotodigital / Getty Images

[Almond milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/does-almond-milk-spike-blood-sugar-11902080) is prepared by soaking almonds in water, then blending and straining out the solids. According to researchers at Monash University, a 1-cup serving is low in FODMAPs.7

Unsweetened almond milk is low in calories and carbohydrates, but also low in protein and other nutrients found in cow's milk.8  Although many brands are fortified with calcium and vitamins A and D, the amounts can vary and some have added sweeteners, so check the nutrition label.

[Almond milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/is-almond-milk-good-for-you-11899116) may be a healthy milk alternative for people with IBS, lactose intolerance, or a milk allergy or intolerance.

4

## Hemp Milk

KarpenkovDenis / Getty Images

Hemp milk is made from hemp seeds and has a nutty flavor. Although hemp seeds are in the same family as marijuana, they are very different plants.

Hemp milk is a good source of:9

- [Omega-3 fatty acids](https://www.verywellhealth.com/omega-3-fatty-acids-8660468)
- Vitamins A, D, B2, and B12
- Calcium (when fortified)
- All nine essential amino acids

Hemp milk offers less protein than cow's milk, providing 3 grams compared to 8 grams. However, it is a plant-based protein source and can be beneficial for vegetarians.

Hemp milk is low in FODMAPs and is an option for vegetarians and people with IBS, lactose intolerance, or a milk allergy or intolerance.

5

## Coconut Milk

Anastasiya Larionova / Getty Images

[Coconut milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/coconut-milk-7971556) is made from coconut meat. It is a good fiber source and contains many vitamins and minerals. However, it is high in [saturated fats](https://www.verywellhealth.com/difference-between-saturated-fats-and-unsaturated-fats-697517), which may increase cardiovascular disease risk if consumed in high amounts.

However, coconut milk contains lauric acid and medium-chain triglycerides which may promote good health.10

If you have IBS, the portion size for [coconut milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/foods-on-the-low-fodmap-diet-1944679) is important. According to the Monash University app, a serving size should be limited to 1/2 cup.

Many commercial coconut milks have guar gum added. Guar gum is not a FODMAP but may have a laxative effect. It is unclear if the coconut milk tested at Monash University contained guar gum.

Like almond and hemp milk, coconut milk is easier to make at home than you might think. If you make your own coconut milk, you won't have to worry about added ingredients.

6

## Rice Milk

bhofack2 / Getty Images

[Rice milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/the-best-milks-for-your-belly-1944752) is made by soaking rice, grinding it, and mixing it with water. It is then filtered to remove the solids. Rice milk is low in protein and fiber and most store-bought versions of rice milk are fortified with calcium, vitamin D, and vitamin B12 at similar levels to cow's milk.11

Rice milk is low in FODMAPs and is an option for people with IBS, lactose intolerance, or a milk allergy or intolerance.

7

## Oat Milk

alvarez / Getty Images

Oat milk is made by blending oats with water into a fine consistency. Enzymes are added to break down the oat starch and then it is strained to remove the oat pulp, leaving behind the liquid base.

Oat milk is naturally sweet from the oats and contains soluble fiber, which makes it creamier. Nutritionally oat milk lacks certain amino acids, calcium, and vitamin A [compared with cow's milk](https://www.verywellhealth.com/healthiest-milks-11924139).12 The protein content is similar to hemp milk with about 3 grams per 8-ounce serving.13

Oat milk is low in FODMAPs and is an option for vegetarians and people with IBS, lactose intolerance, or a milk allergy or intolerance.

8

## Cashew Milk

george tsartsianidis / Getty Images

Although cashews are a high FODMAP food, cashew milk is made from only a few cashews ground together with water and strained, so it is considered low in FODMAPs.

Unsweetened cashew milk is low in calories, but it is also low in protein and fiber compared to cow's milk.14 It may be fortified with calcium and vitamins A and D, but the amounts can vary by brand and some contain added sweeteners, so check the nutrition label carefully.

Cashew milk is low in FODMAPs and is an option for people with IBS, lactose intolerance, or a milk allergy or intolerance.

9

## Kefir

Ekaterina Fedotova / 500px / Getty Images

[Kefir](https://www.verywellhealth.com/kefir-benefits-11730069) is a fermented milk drink typically made from the milk of cows, sheep, or goats. It can also be cultivated from coconut, soy, and rice milks. As a fermented food, kefir is filled with beneficial [probiotic bacteria](https://www.verywellhealth.com/probiotics-7488911) and yeast.

Kefir is thicker than regular milk but much [thinner than yogurt](https://www.verywellhealth.com/kefir-vs-yogurt-11727338). It has a pleasant, tangy flavor.

Kefir may do more than help you avoid digestive symptoms. It may actually improve the health of your digestive system.15

Kefir has not yet been tested at Monash University for its FODMAP count. However, the fermentation process results in a low-lactose beverage. For that reason, it may be well tolerated by those who have IBS.7
