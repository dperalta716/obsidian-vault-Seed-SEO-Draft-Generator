## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Baseline Summary:

Top-ranking articles for "best immune support supplement" balance evidence-based supplement information with strong emphasis on food-first approaches and lifestyle factors. The most successful articles consistently cover the core trio (Vitamin C, D, Zinc) in deep detail with academic citations, then expand to herbal supplements like elderberry, echinacea, and garlic with medium-depth coverage.

The competitive landscape shows a spectrum from heavily cited medical sites (Healthline ~2800 words, GoodRx ~2400 words) to more skeptical institutional voices (Harvard Health ~1800 words) to product-focused consumer guides (BBC Good Food ~2200 words). Cleveland Clinic takes a more focused approach at ~1200 words, covering only vitamins and minerals.

All articles use hedged language ("may help," "research suggests") around supplement efficacy and include consistent warnings about consulting healthcare providers. Citation density varies significantly—Healthline and GoodRx cite approximately 1 source per 100 words, while Harvard Health relies more on institutional authority with minimal inline citations.

### Gaps & Opportunities for Seed:

- **Gap 1**: Limited coverage of gut-immune connection mechanisms beyond simple probiotic mentions—opportunity for Seed's microbiome expertise
- **Gap 2**: No articles discuss supplement quality, bioavailability, or form differences—opportunity for precision/evidence-based formulation angle
- **Gap 3**: Minimal discussion of synergistic effects between nutrients or comprehensive formulation approach
- **Gap 4**: Few articles address timing/cycling of immune supplements or individual variation in needs
- **Opportunity**: Layer Seed's unique perspective on precision supplementation, microbiome-immune axis, and evidence-based formulation quality

### Recommended Approach:

1. **Meet baseline** by covering: Vitamin C, Vitamin D, Zinc (deep), Elderberry, Probiotics/Gut health (medium), food-first philosophy, safety considerations
2. **Layer Seed perspective** via: Microbiome-immune connection expertise, bioavailable forms, synergistic formulation approach, precision dosing philosophy
3. **Target word count**: 1800-2000 words (baseline articles range: 1200-2800, median ~2000)
4. **Citation strategy**: Aim for ~12-15 academic sources based on competitive patterns; use studies from claims documents as primary authority
