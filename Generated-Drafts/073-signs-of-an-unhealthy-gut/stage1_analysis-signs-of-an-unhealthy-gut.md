# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: Signs of an Unhealthy Gut

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary Intent**: Users want a clear, trustworthy checklist of symptoms that indicate their gut health may be compromised — and actionable steps to fix it.

**Core Questions Users Are Trying to Answer**:
1. "What symptoms mean my gut is unhealthy?"
2. "Could my [fatigue / skin issues / mood changes] be related to my gut?"
3. "What can I actually do to improve my gut health?"

**Intent Type**: Informational with solution-seeking — users want to identify the problem AND find remedies. The "what you can do about it" angle is critical.

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Opportunity Assessment

**The competitive landscape is exceptionally weak:**
1. Zero academic citations across 5 articles (1 PubMed link total)
2. Uniformly shallow — no article goes deep on mechanisms
3. Generic solutions with no evidence-based specificity
4. No expert quotes from researchers
5. Most are thinly veiled lead-gen pieces
6. Word counts are short (800-2,200 words)
7. No one discusses probiotic survivability, clinical trial data, or gut barrier mechanisms

### Seed's Differentiation Strategy

**Evidence Advantage**: Even 10-12 citations would represent 10x more evidence than the entire competitive set combined.

**Solutions Depth**: Per user instructions, our solutions section must be MORE exhaustive and evidence-based than competitors. This means:
- Specific dietary recommendations with evidence
- Fiber types and why they matter
- Prebiotics explained with mechanism
- Probiotics with clinical trial evidence (DS-01 Allegretti trial)
- Stress management with gut-brain axis science
- Sleep hygiene connected to serotonin/melatonin pathway
- Movement with microbiome diversity data

**Seed's Unique Angles**:
1. **Root-cause vs. band-aid**: Competitors list symptoms and generic fixes. Seed can explain WHY these symptoms happen (gut barrier integrity, microbiome diversity loss, SCFA deficiency) and what actually addresses the root causes.
2. **Probiotic precision**: Competitors say "take probiotics" with no specificity. Seed can contrast this with clinically validated, survivability-tested formulations.
3. **Engineered to Survive**: When competitors mention fermented foods as probiotic sources, Seed can raise the question of whether those microbes survive digestion — a natural bridge to ViaCap.
4. **Clinical evidence**: The Allegretti 2026 trial (n=350) provides a concrete evidence anchor that no competitor has anything comparable to.
5. **Gut barrier science**: No competitor discusses gut barrier integrity. Seed can explain how a compromised barrier contributes to multiple symptoms (skin, mood, immune).
