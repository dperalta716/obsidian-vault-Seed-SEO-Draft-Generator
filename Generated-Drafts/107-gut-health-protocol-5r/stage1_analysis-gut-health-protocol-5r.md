# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: Gut Health Protocol (5R)

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary Intent:** Informational -- users want to understand what the 5R gut health protocol is, its five steps (Remove, Replace, Reinoculate, Repair, Rebalance), whether it works, and how to apply it.

**Core Questions Users Are Trying to Answer:**
1. What is the 5R protocol and what does each step involve?
2. Does the 5R gut healing protocol actually work (is there evidence)?
3. How do I implement each step practically (foods, actions)?
4. Is this protocol appropriate for me (healthy person vs. diagnosed condition)?
5. What role do probiotics play in the 5R framework?

**Search Context:** This keyword sits at the intersection of functional medicine and consumer gut health interest. Searchers range from wellness-curious consumers who have heard about the 5R protocol on social media or podcasts (Dr. Hyman's podcast episode is ranking) to practitioners looking for a refresher framework.

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Landscape Summary
- All 5 competitor articles present the 5R protocol uncritically as a proven, structured framework
- NONE question whether the protocol as a whole has been clinically tested (major gap)
- Citation quality is low across the board -- WellTheory is the only article with meaningful academic references
- Average word count is ~800 words; WellTheory at ~1500 is the longest
- No article differentiates between evidence-backed steps (probiotics for Reinoculate) and poorly-evidenced steps (enzymes for Replace in healthy people)

### Seed's Differentiation Opportunity (Aligned with SciCare POV)
The SciCare POV brief provides a major angle no competitor takes: **the 5R protocol as a unified framework has never been formally tested.** Individual components have varying evidence quality:
- **Strongest evidence:** Reinoculate step (probiotics + gut barrier) -- meta-analysis support
- **Conditional evidence:** Repair step (glutamine only at higher doses, shorter windows)
- **Weakest evidence:** Replace step (enzyme supplementation outside diagnosed deficiency)
- **Remove step concern:** Elimination diets can reduce microbiome diversity in healthy people

This honest, evidence-weighted analysis aligns perfectly with Seed's "Inspiring Scientist" voice -- cutting through the functional medicine hype to present what the science actually says.

### DS-01 Positioning Strategy
- **Topic Appropriateness:** This is a general gut health/wellness protocol topic -- DS-01 can be mentioned naturally in the context of the Reinoculate step, which has the strongest evidence
- **Key Angle:** The Reinoculate step is where the strongest evidence lives, and this is where clinically validated probiotics play a direct role
- **Relevant Claims:** Bloating + gas reduction, gut barrier support, increases beneficial bacteria -- all directly relevant to what the 5R protocol aims to achieve
- **Differentiation:** Strain specificity and survivability matter -- generic "take a probiotic" advice misses the nuance that not all probiotics survive digestion or have clinical evidence behind them
