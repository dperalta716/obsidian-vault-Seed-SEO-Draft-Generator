## Abstract Summary

NNS may alter the gut microbiome, but findings remain inconsistent. Animal studies often report a decrease in beneficial bacteria like Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus, and an increase in harmful strains such as Clostridium difficile and E. coli, potentially leading to inflammation and gut imbalance. Disruptions in short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) production and gut hormone signaling have also been observed. Human studies generally show milder or no significant changes.

## Key Findings

### Six FDA-Approved NNS
| NNS | FDA Approval Year | ADI |
|---|---|---|
| Advantame | 2014 | 32.8 mg/kg bw/d |
| Neotame | 2002 | 0.3 mg/kg bw/d |
| Sucralose | 1998 | 5 mg/kg bw/d |
| Saccharin | 1977 | 15 mg/kg bw/d |
| Aspartame | 1981 | 50 mg/kg bw/d |
| Acesulfame K | 1988 | 15 mg/kg bw/d |

Stevia (steviol glycosides) and rebaudioside A are FDA-recognized as GRAS (not approved food additives — a distinction worth noting).

### Effects on Gut Microbiome
- Animal studies: NNS reduce beneficial bacteria (Bifidobacterium, Lactobacillus); increase harmful strains (Clostridium difficile, E. coli)
- SCFA production disruptions observed
- Maternal sucralose intake alters gut microbiome makeup and diversity, reduces butyrate-producing bacteria
- Human studies: generally milder or no significant changes
- Dysbiosis linked to: inflammation, metabolic disturbances, increased susceptibility to IBD and type 2 diabetes

### Notable by Sweetener Type
- Saccharin: Inhibitory effects on beneficial bacteria including Lactobacillus and Akkermansia muciniphila; glucose intolerance in animal models; human studies suggest gut microbial imbalances
- Sucralose: >85% reaches colon; animal studies show significant decrease in beneficial bacteria; maternal exposure affects offspring microbiome
- Aspartame: Rapidly metabolized in small intestine; theoretically minimal direct gut microbiota interaction; animal studies show altered diversity; human evidence mixed
- Acesulfame-K: Conflicting results; rapid urinary excretion limits colonic interaction
