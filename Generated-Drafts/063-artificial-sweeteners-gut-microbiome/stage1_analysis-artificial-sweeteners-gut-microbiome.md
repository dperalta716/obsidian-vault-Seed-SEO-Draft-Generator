## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Seed's Unique Angles
1. **The definition boundary nobody draws clearly**: Most competitors either (a) lump all sweetener types together (including stevia and sugar alcohols) or (b) treat them all equivalently. Seed can own the precise taxonomy — artificial/synthetic vs. natural non-nutritive vs. sugar alcohols — and explain WHY it matters for microbiome research.

2. **The animal-to-human gap is the story nobody tells well**: The most alarming findings (saccharin inducing glucose intolerance in mice) involved very high doses and animal models. Human RCTs show a more nuanced picture. An honest assessment here is itself a differentiator.

3. **The mechanism → DS-01 bridge is tighter than competitors do**: Competitors say "gut bacteria are disrupted." Seed can trace the exact pathway: reduced diversity → reduced butyrate production → weakened gut barrier → glucose dysregulation — and then bridge EACH mechanism to what DS-01 formulation is designed to support (increases beneficial bacteria, supports gut barrier integrity per Claims Library).

4. **The carrier compound nuance (sucralose)**: Powdered sucralose products often use maltodextrin as a carrier, which has its own microbiome effects. This is a genuine nuance not covered by most consumer-facing competitors.
