# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: L-Glutamine for Gut Health

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary intent**: Informational -- users want to understand whether L-glutamine actually helps gut health, how it works, and whether they should take it.

**Core questions users are trying to answer:**
- Does L-glutamine really support gut health, or is it overhyped?
- How does L-glutamine help the gut lining specifically?
- How much should I take and for how long?
- Is it safe, and are there side effects?
- Can I combine L-glutamine with probiotics?

## 2. Competitive Baseline Requirements

**Must-cover topics (in 3+ articles):**
- What L-glutamine is (amino acid definition, conditionally essential)
- Gut lining / tight junction mechanisms
- Dietary sources of glutamine
- Dosage guidance (5-15g/day range)
- Side effects and contraindications
- Evidence caveats (mixed results for healthy people)
- IBS/IBD relevance

**Common in 2+ articles:**
- L-glutamine vs D-glutamine distinction
- Supplement forms (powder vs capsule)
- Timing recommendations
- Sickle cell disease (FDA-approved use)
- Cancer dual-role concerns

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Standard Advice to Challenge
1. "Take 5-15g of L-glutamine daily to heal your gut" -- oversimplified; dosing depends on context
2. "L-glutamine can fix leaky gut on its own" -- ignores the microbial ecosystem entirely
3. "Everyone should take L-glutamine for gut health" -- healthy bodies produce enough

### Seed's Unique Angles
1. **Structural vs. ecosystem approach**: L-glutamine feeds the walls; probiotics tend the community inside. Neither alone provides complete gut support.
2. **Complementary mechanisms**: L-glutamine + probiotics address gut health from two entirely different angles -- that's the real story competitors miss.
3. **Clinical validation gap**: Most L-glutamine studies are small or preclinical. DS-01's Allegretti trial (n=350) represents a different evidentiary standard.
4. **Root-cause framework**: L-glutamine supports the gut barrier structurally, but DS-01 transforms the microbiome and targets root causes of digestive distress.

### Differentiation Opportunities
- NO competitor article discusses L-glutamine + probiotics as complementary approaches in depth
- NO competitor uses expert quotes or warm conversational tone
- Most rely on hedging without providing actionable perspective
- AFPA is the most comprehensive but tonally flat -- opportunity to match depth with Seed's voice
- Target: 1800-2000 words with 12-15 academic citations
