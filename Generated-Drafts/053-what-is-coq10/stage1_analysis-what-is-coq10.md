## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Competitive Baseline Summary:

The top-ranking content for "what is coq10" is dominated by authoritative medical institutions (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, NIH/PMC, British Heart Foundation) that prioritize clinical accuracy and safety information. Articles range from 800-2100 words, with most falling between 1100-1500 words. The sweet spot appears to be 1200-1600 words.

The competitive landscape requires strong scientific credibility, clear explanations of biochemistry, and balanced coverage of both benefits and safety considerations. All successful articles lead with a clear definition, emphasize mitochondrial energy production, and dedicate significant space to heart health benefits.

Most content is clinical and authoritative in tone but lacks warmth or accessibility. Citation density is surprisingly low across most sources, with only the PMC academic article providing robust DOI links.

### Gaps & Opportunities for Seed:

- **Gap 1**: Most articles lack complete DOI citations - opportunity to differentiate with rigorous sourcing
- **Gap 2**: Limited coverage of ubiquinol vs ubiquinone forms and bioavailability differences
- **Gap 3**: Fertility benefits underexplored across most sources
- **Gap 4**: No articles deeply cover the microbiome connection to CoQ10
- **Gap 5**: Age-related decline discussed superficially - opportunity for deeper explanation
- **Gap 6**: Exercise performance and cognitive benefits receive light treatment
- **Opportunity**: Most content is clinical/dry - opportunity for more engaging, accessible tone while maintaining authority

### Recommended Approach:

1. **Meet baseline** by covering: CoQ10 definition, ATP/energy production, antioxidant function, heart health benefits, statin interaction, dosage guidance, dietary sources, safety/side effects
2. **Layer Seed perspective** via: Bioavailable forms discussion, precision dosing philosophy, microbiome connection if relevant to Seed products
3. **Target word count**: 1500-1800 words (baseline articles range: 800-2100, sweet spot: 1200-1600)
4. **Citation strategy**: Aim for ~12-15 academic sources with complete DOIs - significantly exceed competitor citation quality
5. **Tone differentiation**: Maintain scientific authority while adding accessible, conversational warmth that competitors lack
