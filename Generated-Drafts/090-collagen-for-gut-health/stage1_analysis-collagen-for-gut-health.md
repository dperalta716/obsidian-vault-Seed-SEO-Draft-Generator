## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### SciCare POV Brief (Governs Article Angle)
**Found:** `Reference/SciCare POV briefs/2026-05-13/collagen for gut health.md`
**Key takeaway:** Early research suggests collagen peptides may influence gut barrier function, digestive symptoms, and the gut microbiome, but evidence in human clinical trials is limited, and effects likely depend heavily on dose, context, and individual health status.
**Narrative backbone:** Balance the promise with the limitations. Do NOT present collagen as a universal gut health solution. Emphasize context-dependency.

### Standard Advice to Challenge
1. "Collagen repairs leaky gut" — Most competitors state this definitively, but evidence is preliminary and not placebo-controlled
2. "Just take collagen for gut health" — Competitors present collagen as standalone solution, ignoring the microbial dimension entirely
3. "All collagen types are equally effective" — No head-to-head comparison data exists; hydrolyzed peptides are the only form studied for gut benefits

### Seed's Differentiation Angle
- Collagen addresses the structural side of gut health (amino acids → lining → tight junctions)
- The microbial side (microbiome diversity, SCFA production, immune engagement) needs its own approach
- DS-01® addresses this microbial dimension with clinical validation for bloating, gas, and regularity
- Frame as "different mechanisms, different tools" — NOT as "collagen is incomplete, buy DS-01"

### Evidence Strategy
- **Tier 0 (SciCare POV):** Abrahams 2022, Wu 2025, Ren 2024, Li 2022
- **Tier 1 (Claims Library — DS-01 only):** Allegretti 2026
- **Tier 3 (Pre-vetted):** Chen 2017, Sperber 2021, Mei 2020, Zihni 2016, + additional verified sources
- **Target:** 12 unique sources, ~1 citation per 75-100 words for key claims

### Recommended Article Structure
1. Overview (3-5 bullet summary)
2. Introduction (hook + direct answer + nuance per SciCare POV)
3. H2: What Collagen Actually Does in Your Gut (amino acids, tight junctions, prebiotic-like effects)
4. H2: What the Research Shows—And Where It Gets Complicated (Abrahams study + limitations + Li cautionary finding)
5. H2: The Microbial Side of Gut Health (probiotics as different mechanism → DS-01 in separate paragraph)
6. H2: How to Use Collagen for Your Gut (hydrolyzed peptides, dose, food sources, bigger picture)
7. The Key Insight (balanced summary, warm close)
8. FAQs (3 PAA questions)
