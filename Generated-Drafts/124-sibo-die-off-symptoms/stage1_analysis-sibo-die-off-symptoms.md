# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: SIBO die-off symptoms

> **SciCare POV brief loaded** (`Reference/SciCare POV briefs/2026-06-17/sibo-die-off-symptoms.md`). This brief governs ANGLE and primary evidence. CRITICAL flag from SciCare: pairing "SIBO" with "die-off symptoms" is **scientifically unsupported** — there is no controlled evidence that a Herxheimer-type die-off reaction occurs in SIBO. The one well-documented form of "die-off" is the **Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction** seen when antibiotics treat spirochetal infections (syphilis, Lyme, leptospirosis). The article's job is to honor the search intent while correcting the extrapolation — explaining what die-off actually is, where it's real, and why the SIBO/probiotic/cleanse versions are not confirmed by science.

## 1. User Search Intent

People searching "SIBO die-off symptoms" are mid-treatment (or about to start) and feel rough. Their real questions:
- **What is "die-off" and is what I'm feeling normal/expected?** (reassurance + identification)
- **What symptoms count as die-off?** (fatigue, headache, brain fog, flu-like, worsened bloating)
- **How long will it last?** (onset/duration)
- **What helps / how do I manage it?** (practical relief)
- **Does die-off mean treatment is working?** (validation)

Secondary intent: distinguishing die-off from a treatment that isn't working, a virus, or a bad reaction.

**The honest gap none of the competitors fill:** whether "SIBO die-off" is a scientifically established phenomenon at all. Every competitor assumes it is. SciCare's POV is that it is not confirmed — and that's the differentiated, trustworthy angle.
