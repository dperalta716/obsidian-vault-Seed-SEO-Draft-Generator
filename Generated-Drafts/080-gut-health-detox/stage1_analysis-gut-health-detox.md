# Stage 1 Pre-Draft Analysis: Gut Health Detox

## 1. User Search Intent

**Primary intent:** Users want actionable steps to "detox" or "cleanse" their gut — they believe their gut needs a reset and are looking for methods (dietary, supplemental, or procedural) to do so.

**Core questions:**
- How do I detox my gut? (step-by-step methods)
- Is gut detoxing actually necessary or effective?
- What foods or drinks help cleanse the gut?
- How long does a gut detox take?
- Are gut cleanses safe?

**Intent nuance:** The search blends two audiences — (1) people seeking specific detox protocols/products, and (2) people curious whether "gut detox" is real science. Seed's article should serve both by educating on what the science actually says while providing evidence-based alternatives.

## 6. Synthesis for Drafting

### Seed's Competitive Advantages

1. **Evidence authority:** The SciCare POV provides 6 pre-vetted academic sources + 5 additional inline references that NO competitor uses. This is the single biggest differentiator.

2. **The microbiome disruption angle:** Research shows gut cleanses *disrupt* the very microbial balance they claim to restore (PMC10265323). Zero competitors cite this. This is Seed's knockout punch.

3. **Specific harm evidence:** Case reports of liver injury (Niazi 2022), kidney injury, electrolyte imbalances from detox products. Competitors mention risks vaguely; Seed can cite specific cases.

4. **RCT evidence:** Tinsley et al. 2019 is a double-blind RCT showing a detox supplement performed identically to placebo. No competitor cites this.

5. **FDA/FTC enforcement:** The NCCIH reference documents federal action against detox products. Powerful credibility signal.

### SciCare POV Throughline

**Core narrative:** "Your body already detoxes itself. Commercial gut cleanses not only don't work — they can actively harm the microbiome they claim to help. What your gut actually needs is consistent daily nourishment, not periodic purging."

### Standard Advice to Contrast

1. **"Do a 3-day/7-day gut cleanse"** → SciCare: No evidence these work, and they can disrupt microbial diversity
2. **"Drink detox teas for gut health"** → SciCare: Detox teas lack human evidence, may contain harmful stimulants, have caused liver injury
3. **"Use colon cleanses to remove toxins"** → SciCare: No rigorous trials support this; risks include electrolyte imbalances, kidney injury, infection
