## What Makes a Multivitamin Actually Work?
Before we compare the bottles, let’s look at the criteria that actually matter once you get past the marketing.

1.  **Bioavailability**
    You might assume that 400 mcg of folic acid is the same in every bottle, but your body disagrees. "Bioavailability" refers to how easily your body can absorb and utilize a nutrient. Many standard multivitamins use synthetic forms (like folic acid or cyanocobalamin) because they are shelf-stable and affordable. However, your body often has to convert these into "active" forms before they can be used—a process that isn't efficient for everyone, especially those with MTHFR gene variations.

2.  **Delivery**
    It’s not just about what you take; it’s about where it goes. For gummies, the delivery system is a gelatin or pectin matrix, which usually requires added sugar to mask the metallic taste of vitamins. For capsules, the technology can determine if nutrients survive stomach acid to reach the small intestine or colon where they are absorbed best.

3.  **The Microbiome Paradigm Shift**
    This is where the science has evolved significantly. We used to think nutrition was just about feeding your human cells. Now, we know it’s about feeding your microbiome, too. Your gut bacteria actually produce certain B vitamins and vitamin K themselves, but they need the right "fuel" to do so ([Magnúsdóttir 2015](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148)). A truly effective multivitamin considers this ecosystem, delivering nutrients that support both you and your resident microbes.

### Considerations
If you have trouble swallowing pills, this is a very user-friendly option. However, the trade-off is the use of synthetic vitamin forms, the lack of iron, and the inclusion of added sugar and gelatin.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why don't Vitafusion and OLLY contain iron?
Iron has a strong, metallic taste that is very difficult to mask in a gummy format. It can also cause stomach upset for some people. Because gummies are designed for taste and texture, most brands, including Vitafusion and OLLY, leave iron out. If you need iron, you would need to take a separate supplement or choose a capsule-based multivitamin.

### Is the sugar in gummy vitamins a problem?
It depends on your goals. While 2-3 grams of sugar isn't a massive amount (it's less than a teaspoon), it is still added sugar that you are consuming daily. For some, the bigger issue is that the sugar and gelatin matrix takes up space in the gummy that *could* be used for more nutrients, limiting how comprehensive the formula can be.

### Can I take Seed DM-02™ if I have the MTHFR gene variation?
Many people with MTHFR variations look for methylated forms of B vitamins because their bodies struggle to convert synthetic folic acid. DM-02™ is formulated with methylated folate (5-MTHF) and methylcobalamin (B12), which are forms that don't require the same conversion process as the forms found in most gummies.
