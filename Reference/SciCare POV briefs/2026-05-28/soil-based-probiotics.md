## SciCare POV | Soil-Based Probiotics

[Soil-based probiotics](https://doi.org/10.3390/nu18050802) (SBOs) are spore-forming bacteria—commonly *Bacillus* species like *Bacillus coagulans* and *Bacillus subtilis*—that originate from soil and the natural environment and are distinguished by their ability to form protective spores. These spores allow them to survive harsh conditions, including stomach acid and heat, potentially making them more shelf-stable than conventional probiotic strains. Once in the gut, spores germinate into active bacteria that can influence the gut environment. A [review](https://doi.org/10.3390/nu18050802) found that *Bacillus subtilis* supports digestion by producing enzymes that help break down nutrients, strengthens the gut lining by boosting tight junction proteins, and shapes the composition of gut bacteria through what researchers describe as a "gut-centric, multi-system" mechanism. *Bacillus coagulans* has been particularly well-studied in humans—one [trial](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodres.2021.110428) found it improved gut transit time and stool consistency in adults with mild constipation, alongside shifts in gut bacteria composition.

Human clinical trials support SBOs for several GI complaints. *Bacillus coagulans* significantly [reduced](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000033109) gas, bloating, and indigestion symptoms compared to placebo in a multicenter trial of healthy adults. Multiple randomized controlled trials in adults with IBS found that *Bacillus coagulans* strains improved [abdominal pain](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000039134) and [stool consistency](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000023641) with no serious adverse events. A pilot trial using a combination of *Bacillus coagulans* MY01 and *Bacillus subtilis* MY02 found [clinical responders](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2468-1253\(21\)00226-0) were nearly twice as likely in the probiotic group compared to placebo in adults with functional dyspepsia. A dual-strain formula combining *Bacillus subtilis* and a closely related *Heyndrickxia* species also [improved abdominal pain](https://doi.org/10.1163/18762891-bja00113) and stool consistency in adults with diarrhea-predominant IBS. Safety profiles across these trials were consistently favorable, with no reported serious adverse events.

**Key Takeaway:** Soil-based probiotics, particularly *Bacillus coagulans* and *Bacillus subtilis*, have the strongest evidence for supporting gut health—including relief from bloating, gas, constipation, and IBS symptoms—and their spore-forming nature may give them a stability advantage over conventional probiotic strains.
