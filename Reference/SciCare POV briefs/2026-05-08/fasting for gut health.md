## SciCare POV | Fasting + Gut Health

Emerging research suggests that fasting patterns—including intermittent fasting and time-restricted eating (TRE)—may influence the [gut microbiome](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnut.2024.1342787), [metabolic health](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMra1905136), and [gastrointestinal function](https://doi.org/10.3390/nu15020259). Some studies have observed shifts in [microbial diversity](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nut.2020.110797), microbial metabolite production, [circadian rhythm alignment](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gastha.2021.10.008), and markers related to gut barrier integrity or inflammation. However, the field is still evolving, and much of the mechanistic evidence comes from animal or preclinical models rather than long-term human clinical trials.

Overall, the current body of evidence suggests fasting *may* make a meaningful difference for certain individuals, particularly through indirect mechanisms such as meal timing, dietary consistency, metabolic changes, and circadian alignment. At the same time, [microbiome responses](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2021.642999) appear highly individualized, and it remains difficult to isolate the effects of fasting itself from accompanying changes in calorie intake, food quality, sleep, or weight loss. In other words, fasting is not currently understood as a universal or standalone intervention for "gut health," but rather one of several lifestyle factors that may shape the gut microbiome and digestive physiology.

## Suggested References

### 1. Effects of Intermittent Fasting on Health, Aging, and Disease

De Cabo R, Mattson MP | New England Journal of Medicine (2019)

- **Article Type:** Narrative review
- **Population/Demographic:** Broad overview of animal and human research
- **Open Access:** No (abstract freely available)
- **DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMra1905136](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMra1905136)

**Why it's useful:**
A review summarizing the physiological effects of intermittent fasting, including metabolic health, inflammation, circadian biology, and emerging microbiome-related mechanisms. Helpful for broad context, though not specifically focused on gut microbiome outcomes.

---

### 2. Circadian Rhythms, the Gut Microbiota, and Metabolic Disorders

Zhao E, et al. | Cell Host & Microbe (2022)

- **Article Type:** Mechanistic review + preclinical discussion
- **Population/Demographic:** Primarily animal/preclinical research with translational human implications
- **Open Access:** Yes
- **DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gastha.2021.10.008](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gastha.2021.10.008)

**Why it's useful:**
A foundational paper supporting the idea that meal timing and circadian rhythms may shape microbiome activity. Particularly relevant when discussing TRE and the gut microbiome, though translation to humans is still being investigated.

---

### 3. The impact of intermittent fasting on gut microbiota: a systematic review of human studies

Paukkonen I, et al. | Frontiers in Nutrition (2024)

- **Article Type:** Systematic review
- **Population/Demographic:** Human
- **Open Access:** Yes
- **DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3389/fnut.2024.1342787](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnut.2024.1342787)

**Why it's useful:**
Provides a broad overview of current human research on intermittent fasting and the gut microbiome (looked at 8 trials), including time-restricted eating, alternate-day fasting, and 5:2 approaches. Helpful for illustrating that fasting patterns may influence microbial richness, diversity, and composition, while also emphasizing that findings remain highly variable and the field is still evolving.

Zeb F, et al. | Nutrients (2023)

- **Article Type:** Review
- **Population/Demographic:** Mechanistic/animal/human studies overview
- **Open Access:** Yes

Provides a broad overview of how time-restricted eating may interact with the gut microbiome, circadian rhythms, metabolism, and immune signaling. Zeb F, et al. | Nutrients (2020)

- **Open Access:** Yes
- **DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nut.2020.110797](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nut.2020.110797)

### 7. Effects of Ramadan intermittent fasting on gut microbiome: is the diet key?

Saglam D, et al. | Frontiers in Microbiology (2023)

- **Article Type:** Small Study (n=12)
- **Population/Demographic:** Human Ramadan fasting
- **Open Access:** Yes
- **DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1203205](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1203205)

Provides human data showing that prolonged daily fasting patterns may influence gut microbiome composition. Particularly useful for illustrating that microbiome responses to fasting appear to depend not only on fasting duration, but also on the types of foods consumed during eating periods.
