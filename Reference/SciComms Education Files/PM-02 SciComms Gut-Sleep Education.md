## **ğŸ’¤ Melatonin Deep Dive**

**High doses of melatonin, particularly in the range of 5 to 10 mg or more, can disrupt natural sleep cycles and lead to various side effects.**

**Melatonin is a hormone naturally produced by the pineal gland, playing a crucial role in regulating the sleep-wake cycle. Supplementation with melatonin is commonly used to address sleep disorders, however the dosage is critical. Studies have shown that lower [doses](https://www.ingentaconnect.com/content/ascp/tscp/2019/00000034/00000007/art00003;jsessionid=m1hqfn3sw5gs.x-ic-live-01), typically between 0.5 to 5 mg, are effective for improving sleep onset and quality. Exceeding this range does not necessarily enhance efficacy and may increase the risk of adverse effects. High doses can lead to elevated melatonin levels in the bloodstream, potentially causing a phase shift in the circadian rhythm, resulting in disrupted sleep patterns, daytime drowsiness/grogginess, and (potentially) dependency.**

**Common [side effects](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jpi.12782) [associated](https://www.dovepress.com/current-insights-into-the-risks-of-using-melatonin-as-a-treatment-for--peer-reviewed-fulltext-article-CIA) with higher doses of melatonin include:**

* **Headaches**

* **Dizziness**

* **Nausea**

* **Vivid dreams or nightmares**

* **Alterations in heart rhythm.**

**These effects are more pronounced with doses exceeding 10 mg per day. Additionally, melatonin can interact with various [medications](https://www.dovepress.com/current-insights-into-the-risks-of-using-melatonin-as-a-treatment-for--peer-reviewed-fulltext-article-CIA) (such as blood pressure drugs and anticoagulants), potentially leading to compounded side effects.**

**The long-term use of high-dose melatonin supplements lacks comprehensive research, raising concerns about potential hormonal disruptions and other health implications.**

**Given these findings, lower doses and sustained release are better choices. These doses [tend to be](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30107892/) well tolerated with limited adverse, withdrawal or â€˜hangoverâ€™ effects, and with limited safety concerns or interactions with medications.**
