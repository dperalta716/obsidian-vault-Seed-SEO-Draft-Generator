## **What Makes a Multivitamin Actually Work?**

Before taking a look at each product, it helps to understand the factors that separate a multivitamin that delivers from one that just checks boxes.

### **Bioavailability: Can Your Body Actually Use It?**

This is the scientific term for “does this vitamin do anything once you swallow it?” Many standard multivitamins use synthetic or inactive forms because they’re cheaper and more shelf-stable. The trade-off? Your body has to convert them before it can use them.

Take **folic acid**—the synthetic form of Vitamin B9. Your body needs to convert it into methylfolate before it’s useful. For most people, this conversion works fine. But for those with certain genetic variations (like MTHFR, which affects up to 40% of the population), this conversion is inefficient. [1](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11930790/#:~:text=Genetic%20variations%2C%20such%20as%20the,levels%20and%20associated%20health%20risks.)

**🔬 Science Translation:** Think of synthetic vs. bioavailable nutrients like furniture that requires assembly vs. furniture that arrives ready to use. Bioavailable forms are the “no tools required” version—though most people can handle the assembly just fine.

### **Delivery: Where Do the Nutrients Actually Go?**

A standard capsule or gummy dissolves in your stomach. That’s fine for some sturdy nutrients, but others get degraded by stomach acid before they ever reach the small intestine where absorption happens. (Your stomach is basically a pool of hydrochloric acid. Not exactly a _gentle_ environment…)

Some delivery systems are designed to protect sensitive ingredients so they can reach different points in the digestive tract.

### **Multivitamins: How Does the Microbiome Play a Role?**

Nutrition isn’t just about feeding your human cells—it’s about feeding your microbiome, too.

Your gut bacteria aren’t just passive passengers. They’re active participants in your nutrition, capable of producing certain B vitamins and vitamin K themselves. But they need the right inputs to do so. A multivitamin that considers this ecosystem can provide nutrients that support the microbes that support you. [2](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148)

## **A Closer Look: Seed’s DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin**

### **The 2-in-1 Difference: Nutrients for You and Your Microbiome**

Seed designed [DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin](https://seed.com/daily-multivitamin) as a system rather than just a nutrient checklist. It’s a 2-in-1 formula: the outer capsule delivers 20 essential vitamins and minerals to your body, while the inner capsule delivers nutrients and prebiotics to your microbiome.

### **Key DM-02™ Features**

DM-02™ is formulated to provide 100% **Daily Value (DV)** of all the nutrients in its formulation—enough to address common nutrition gaps **without overdoing it with megadoses.**(Many labels note up to 5,000% of a vitamin’s DV, which isn’t exactly useful, as your body excretes the excess. 🚽) Rather than overwhelming your system, this measured-dosing strategy focuses on what most adults actually need.

Seed’s DM-02™ may support several areas, from the heart and immune system to [skin health](https://seed.com/cultured/best-vitamins-for-skin-guide/) and gut barrier integrity.

### **Bioavailability: Nutrient Forms Designed for Direct Absorption**

Seed’s scientists formulated DM-02™ with nutrient forms that don’t require conversion before use:

- **Folate:** Delivers 5-MTHF (methylfolate), not synthetic folic acid.
- **Vitamin B12:** Uses methylcobalamin and adenosylcobalamin, bypassing the need for conversion.
- **Vitamin K2 (MK-7):** A highly bioavailable form often missing from standard multis.
- **Chelated Minerals:** Magnesium, Zinc, and Manganese are bound to amino acids to support absorption. [3](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11467658/)

These choices matter beyond personal absorption. Research suggests that bioavailable micronutrients also help shape the composition of your gut microbiome, creating a positive feedback loop for your health. [4](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8268569/)

### **The Cellular Energy Complex: CoQ10, PQQ, and Spermidine**

This is where DM-02™ differs from standard multivitamins. It includes a Cellular Energy Complex with three compounds not found in most daily formulas:

- **CoQ10:** A compound that [powers your mitochondria](https://seed.com/cultured/a-scientists-guide-to-the-best-mitochondria-supplement-guide/) but naturally declines as you age. [5](https://doi.org/10.3390/biology8020028)
- **PQQ:** A micronutrient shown to support mitochondrial biogenesis—the growth of new mitochondria in your cells. [6](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M109.030130)
- **Spermidine:** A polyamine that supports autophagy, your body’s built-in cellular recycling system. [7](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41556-024-01468-x)

### **The Delivery System: ViaCap® Technology**

DM-02™ uses Seed’s ViaCap® delivery technology—a capsule-in-capsule system with two release points. The outer capsule dissolves in the upper GI tract, releasing vitamins and minerals for absorption in the small intestine. The inner capsule is formulated to resist stomach acid and deliver specific nutrients and prebiotics (like Japanese Wasabi extract) to the lower gut, where they can support the microbiome.

**💊 The intent behind this design:** certain nutrients and prebiotic compounds are sensitive to stomach acid. Staged delivery allows the formula to include ingredients that wouldn’t survive a single-capsule format.

### **Microbiome Support: The Gut-Nutrition Connection**

The inner capsule contains a **Japanese Wasabi prebiotic** and a blend of B vitamins tailored for beneficial gut bacteria, or [probiotics](https://seed.com/cultured/types-of-probiotics-strains-guide/). Since microbes can synthesize their own B vitamins, providing them with the right precursors helps support this natural production line. [2](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148)

### **Considerations for Seed’s Multivitamin**

DM-02™ is a comprehensive option that combines a multivitamin with microbiome support and [cellular energy](https://seed.com/cultured/your-cells-dont-need-hype-they-need-help/) compounds in a single capsule.

The trade-offs are a higher price point ( **$39.99/month**), a capsule format that won’t work for those who struggle swallowing pills, and no iron—which means **pre-menopausal women** with higher iron needs would need to supplement separately.

### **Benefit Claims**

HUM positions Base Control as a foundational product to cover common nutrient gaps and support overall wellness. It’s often marketed alongside their beauty-focused line of targeted products.

### **Delivery Format**

Base Control comes as a standard tablet. While effective for basic delivery, tablets don’t offer the staged release of a capsule-in-capsule system.

### **Considerations for HUM’s Multivitamin**

HUM’s Base Control is a budget-friendly multivitamin ( **~$15/month**) that covers the basics without artificial additives. It’s designed for women who want reliable daily coverage without a complicated routine.

## **A Closer Look: OLLY The Perfect Women’s Multi**

### **Formulation Overview**

OLLY is arguably the most recognizable brand on the shelf, known for colorful, tasty gummy vitamins. The Perfect Women’s Multi is designed for convenience and flavor, targeting people who prefer not to swallow pills or capsules.

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

OLLY prioritizes accessibility and taste, making it one of the most approachable options for people new to supplements or those who dislike swallowing pills.

**Ingredients include:**

- **Synthetic Forms:** OLLY uses folic acid (synthetic B9) and cyanocobalamin (synthetic B12). These require conversion by your body, which works fine for most people but may not be ideal for those with certain genetic variations.
- **Variable Dosages:** The gummy format limits nutrient density. OLLY provides 100% DV for 8 nutrients, with others at lower levels (some B vitamins at just 10% DV).
- **Sugar:** Each serving contains 2g of added sugar (glucose syrup and beet sugar).

**⚛️ Fun Fact:** Gummy vitamins were originally developed for children who couldn’t swallow pills. The adult gummy market exploded in the 2010s when supplement companies realized grown-ups wanted the same tasty convenience.

### **Benefit Claims**

OLLY’s Women’s Multi is designed to support physical and mental energy, [bone health](https://seed.com/cultured/bone-health-supplements-guide/), and the immune system via Vitamin C and Zinc. OLLY also emphasizes [Biotin for hair, skin, and nails](https://seed.com/cultured/biotin-for-hair-and-nails-guide/). Their branding is aspirational and lifestyle-forward, which may appeal to women who want their supplements to feel like self-care rather than a daily chore.

### **Delivery Format**

OLLY’s Women’s Multi comes as **gummies**. The gummy format requires binding agents like glucose syrup and gelatin to hold the shape and mask vitamin taste. Gummies are generally more susceptible to heat and moisture degradation than capsules or tablets.

The chewy matrix also limits what nutrients can be included—certain minerals simply don’t remain stable in the format.

### **Considerations for OLLY’s Multivitamin**

OLLY is the compliance-focused option. If the gummy format is what keeps you consistent with daily supplementation, that consistency has real value. The trade-offs are added sugar, synthetic vitamin forms, and variable nutrient density across the formula.

## **The Key Insight**

The format of a multivitamin determines what can go in the bottle. Gummies require taste-masking ingredients like sugar and a stable matrix, which limits certain minerals and nutrient forms. Tablets offer more flexibility but don’t provide staged delivery. Capsule-in-capsule systems allow for different ingredient options—including compounds that wouldn’t survive other formats—but are more expensive.

**OLLY offers accessibility:** a familiar format, low price, and no pills to swallow. For people who struggle with capsules or want a simple daily habit, the gummy format fills that role.

**HUM’s Base Control offers a middle-ground tablet format** with iron included, making it a solid option for those with specific iron needs.

**Seed’s DM-02™ prioritizes bioavailable nutrient forms and includes compounds**(CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine) typically sold separately. The capsule-in-capsule design allows for targeted delivery to both the upper GI tract and lower gut. The trade-off is a higher price and a format that won’t work for everyone.

👉 **The “best” multivitamin** depends on what you’re looking for—consistency, budget, nutrient forms, or more functional ingredients.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **Why Does Seed Cost More Than OLLY or HUM?**

**It combines more ingredients than most competitors.**

Seed’s DM-02™ ( **$39.99**) is a 2-in-1 formulation that combines a multivitamin with a proprietary Microbiome Complex and Cellular Energy Complex (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine). Purchasing these ingredients separately as quality standalone products would typically cost more than the price of DM-02™.

### **Can I Take Multivitamins on an Empty Stomach?**

**It depends on the formula.**

Seed’s DM-02™ uses ViaCap® technology, which is designed to resist stomach acid, releasing nutrients later in the digestive tract—which may reduce the nausea sometimes caused by standard multivitamins.

HUM suggests taking their tablet with food to aid absorption and reduce stomach upset.

OLLY gummies can generally be taken without food, though the sugar content might affect blood glucose if taken alone.

### **Do Multivitamins Have Iron?**

**Some do, many do not.**

HUM’s Base Control contains **18mg of iron** (as ferrous fumarate), designed for pre-menopausal women with higher iron needs. OLLY’s Women’s Multi does not contain iron—the mineral is difficult to mask in gummy flavors.

Seed’s DM-02™ also **excludes iron intentionally**: iron can interfere with the absorption of other minerals in the formula, and most men and postmenopausal women don’t need supplemental iron. [9](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11920315/), [10](https://www.mdpi.com/2036-7481/12/2/33)

If you have an iron deficiency, supplementing separately allows for precise dosing.
