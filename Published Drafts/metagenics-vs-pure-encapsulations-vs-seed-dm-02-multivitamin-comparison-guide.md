## **What Makes a Multivitamin Actually Work?**

Before comparing bottles, it helps to have a clear picture of what actually matters, and what makes a multivitamin worth subscribing to. Most people take a multivitamin as “insurance,” but if your body can’t use what’s inside the capsule, that insurance policy isn’t worth much. (Cue the _womp-womp_ from the sad trombone. 🫠)

### **Bioavailability**

What you absorb matters more than what you swallow. Many standard multivitamins use **“inactive” forms of vitamins (like folic acid)** because they’re cheaper and more stable. The catch? They’re not always “bioavailable.”

Your body has to convert them into **“active” forms (like methylfolate)** before they can be used. For many people, genetic variations make this conversion inefficient, potentially reducing how much benefit they actually get. [1](https://doi.org/10.3109/00498254.2013.845705)

### **Delivery: Capsules, Tablets, Gummies**

Where nutrients end up matters as much as what they are. A tablet without protection can easily dissolve in stomach acid, which could actually destroy certain delicate compounds in the multivitamin.

Advanced delivery systems (like certain capsule technologies) can help to ensure that the nutrients survive digestion and reach the specific part of your GI tract where they can be absorbed most effectively.

### **The Microbiome Factor**

Research on [the gut microbiome](https://seed.com/cultured/what-is-the-gut-microbiome-guide/) is newer territory. Nutrition used to be thought of as feeding your human cells, period. But research suggests it’s a two-way street. Some gut bacteria can produce vitamin K and certain B vitamins themselves, but they need the right fuel to do so. [2](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148)

So, a multivitamin that considers this ecosystem takes a different approach than one that just tries to bypass the gut entirely. (But it’s certainly not a requirement for multivitamins! Just a plus. 😉)

**🔬 Science Translation:** Your gut bacteria aren’t freeloaders—they’re coworkers. Feed them right, and they might actually manufacture some vitamins for you.

## **A Closer Look: Seed’s DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin**

### **The 2-in-1 Approach: Nutrients for You and Your Microbiome**

Seed built its reputation on microbiome science, so it’s no surprise that their multivitamin approaches nutrition supplementation differently than most. It’s designed as a 2-in-1 formula—supporting whole-body health (immune function, [heart health](https://seed.com/cultured/heart-health-supplements-cardiovascular-guide/), [skin clarity](https://seed.com/cultured/best-vitamins-for-skin-guide/)) while simultaneously providing specific nutrients to the gut microbiome.

### **Key DM-02™ Features: What the Science Shows**

DM-02™ provides 100% of the Daily Value for 20 essential vitamins and minerals. The philosophy here is precision dosing—giving your body what it needs to [complement a healthy diet](https://seed.com/cultured/best-foods-gut-microbiome-guide/), rather than mega-doses that often just get excreted. (Because expensive pee doesn’t do anyone any favors. 😬)

### **Bioavailable Forms**

DM-02™ uses active, body-ready forms of nutrients: **methylated B vitamins (like 5-MTHF folate** instead of folic acid), chelated minerals (which are easier to absorb), and Vitamin D3. [3](https://doi.org/10.1024/0300-9831.77.4.243)

These well-absorbed micronutrient forms may also help support your gut microbiome, which research suggests plays a role in overall nutrient status. [4](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19010037)

### **The Cellular Energy Complex: CoQ10, PQQ, and Spermidine**

This is where DM-02™ differs from most supplements and multivitamins. In addition to essential vitamins, it includes a Cellular Energy Complex:

- **Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10):** Important for mitochondrial function; natural levels decline with age. [5](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mad.2021.111521)
- **Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ):** A compound shown to [support mitochondrial biogenesis](https://seed.com/cultured/a-scientists-guide-to-the-best-mitochondria-supplement-guide/)—essentially helping your cells build new energy factories. [6](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.crfs.2024.100889)
- **Spermidine:** A compound that supports autophagy—your cells’ internal recycling process—which plays a role in cellular renewal. [7](https://doi.org/10.1080/15548627.2018.1530929)

### **The Delivery System: ViaCap® Technology**

Seed uses a proprietary capsule-in-capsule technology called ViaCap®:

- **Outer Capsule:** Contains vitamins and minerals in liquid and powder forms, releasing them in the upper GI tract for absorption.
- **Inner Capsule:** Survives the stomach and delivers specific nutrients and prebiotics directly to the colon.

### **Microbiome Support**

The inner capsule of DM-02™ delivers specific B vitamins and [prebiotics](https://seed.com/cultured/what-do-prebiotics-do/) to the lower gut. This supports the bacteria responsible for synthesizing their own vitamins—potentially creating a positive feedback loop for nutrient status.

### **Considerations for Seed’s DM-02™**

DM-02™ is a comprehensive option that combines **precision dosing (exactly 100% DV)**, bioavailable forms, cellular energy compounds, vegan ingredients, and [microbiome support](https://seed.com/cultured/how-to-fix-gut-microbiome-guide/%5C) in a single daily capsule.

**Worth noting:** Seed’s multivitamin sits at a mid-range price point for subscribers ( **$39.99/month**). Additionally, the capsule format may not appeal to those who prefer tablets or gummies.

## **A Closer Look: Metagenics PhytoMulti®**

### **Formulation Overview**

Metagenics’ products are often recommended by integrative and functional medicine practitioners who want higher-potency formulations than what’s typically available at retail. Their PhytoMulti® formula positions itself as a “smart multi” that combines basic vitamins and minerals with concentrated plant compounds.

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

PhytoMulti® uses high-quality forms of vitamins, including **methylated folate (L-5-methyltetrahydrofolate)** and **methylcobalamin (B12)**. It provides a baseline of essential nutrients at high-potency standards typical of practitioner-grade supplements.

### **The Phytonutrient Angle**

PhytoMulti®’s main selling point is its proprietary phytonutrient blend—extracts from 13 different plants, including green tea, pomegranate, and grape seed. The idea is to provide the cellular protection and [antioxidant support](https://seed.com/cultured/best-antioxidant-supplements-guide/) you’d get from a diverse, plant-rich diet.

Metagenics notes that their multivitamin blend may support DNA stability and [overall cellular health](https://seed.com/cultured/your-cells-dont-need-hype-they-need-help/).

### **Delivery Format**

PhytoMulti® comes in a standard tablet format requiring two tablets per day. Tablets are generally more stable for certain compounds and allow for higher doses per serving, but they can be harder to break down than capsules—particularly for people with lower stomach acid or who commonly experience digestive issues.

There’s no specialized delivery technology here; the formula relies on conventional tablet dissolution in the stomach.

### **Considerations for Metagenics PhytoMulti®**

PhytoMulti® was created for people who may be looking for a practitioner-grade quality multivitamin, plus concentrated plant extracts and antioxidant support.

**Worth noting:** it requires two tablets daily for the full serving, carries the highest price point of the three ( **~$44/month**), and doesn’t include mitochondrial compounds like PQQ or targeted microbiome delivery.

## **A Closer Look: Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™**

### **Formulation Overview**

Pure Encapsulations is famous for what they _don’t_ put in their products. O.N.E.™ Multivitamin is a hypoallergenic, “free-from” formula designed for people with sensitivities. It’s a clean, high-quality supplement built around essentials—no proprietary blends, no extras.

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

True to its reputation, O.N.E.™ uses quality nutrient forms: **Metafolin® (a patented form of methylfolate)**, **activated B vitamins (like Riboflavin 5′ phosphate)**, and sustained-release CoQ10—a nice bonus for a basic multivitamin, though it lacks the broader cellular complex found in competitors.

### **The Simplicity Angle**

The primary selling point here is “purity.” O.N.E.™ covers the essentials for antioxidant support, [eye health](https://seed.com/cultured/vitamins-for-eye-health-guide/) (via **Lutein** and **Zeaxanthin**), and cellular function without fillers, gluten, or common allergens.

### **Delivery Format**

As the name suggests, this is a one-a-day capsule—no splitting doses or remembering a second pill. The **capsule format (vegetarian cellulose)** dissolves more readily than tablets for most people and makes it easy to take with or without food. For anyone who’s abandoned a supplement routine because it felt like too much hassle, the simplicity here is a real advantage.

### **Considerations for Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™**

O.N.E.™ is an excellent option for sensitive individuals who need a strictly hypoallergenic formula, or for anyone who wants high-quality basics at the lowest price point ( **~$23/month**).

**Worth noting:** It doesn’t have: prebiotics, specialized capsules designed for the microbiome, or advanced cellular energy compounds like PQQ or spermidine. This is a more traditional approach to multivitamin supplementation.

## **How to Choose: Metagenics vs. Pure Encapsulations vs. Seed DM-02**

**👉 TL;DR:** PhytoMulti® is for the plant-extract enthusiast. O.N.E.™ is for the minimalist with sensitivities. DM-02™ is for the microbiome-first thinker.

### **Choose Metagenics PhytoMulti® If:**

- You want concentrated phytonutrients from 13 plant extracts in addition to essential vitamins
- You want a supplement that doubles as a “greens” boost if your diet is low in fruits and vegetables
- You prefer a practitioner-recommended brand with high-potency formulations
- You don’t mind taking two tablets daily

### **Choose Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™ If:**

- You have allergies or sensitivities and need a strictly hypoallergenic formula
- You want a lower price point ( **~$23/month**)
- You want a streamlined formula without proprietary blends or extras
- You don’t need microbiome-specific support or cellular energy compounds beyond CoQ10

### **Choose Seed DM-02™ If:**

- You want microbiome support built into your multivitamin
- You prefer **precision dosing (100% DV)** rather than mega-doses
- You’re interested in the **Cellular Energy Complex (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine)**
- You want nutrients that survive stomach acid and reach your lower gut intact

## **The Key Insight**

All three multivitamins use bioavailable nutrient forms and avoid cheap synthetic fillers—standard for practitioner-grade supplements. The real question is what _else_ you’re looking for.

PhytoMulti® bets on phytonutrients, packing in plant extracts for antioxidant support. O.N.E.™ bets on simplicity, stripping out allergens and keeping it clean. DM-02™ bets on the microbiome, treating gut bacteria as active participants in nutrition rather than bystanders.

**The “best” multivitamin depends entirely on how you think about health.** If you see your body as a collection of isolated systems, any of these three multivitamins will fill nutrient gaps. If you see it as an interconnected ecosystem—where your gut influences your [energy](https://seed.com/cultured/best-energy-supplements-guide/), immunity, and even your mood—a formula designed with that in mind might be worth the investment.

**🌱 A multivitamin covers micronutrient gaps—but it works best alongside the basics: sleep, movement, and actual vegetables.**

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **Why Do Some Multivitamins Require Multiple Pills Per Day?**

**Effective doses of minerals and bulky plant extracts take up physical space.** Metagenics PhytoMulti® requires two tablets to fit its phytonutrient blend. Seed DM-02™ and Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™ fit into a single daily capsule by prioritizing high-potency micronutrients and precision dosing rather than bulky fillers or macro-minerals typically obtained from food.

### **Can I Take Multivitamins on an Empty Stomach?**

**It’s generally better to take any multivitamin with a meal.** This is especially true for **fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)** and compounds like CoQ10 found in Seed’s DM-02™ and Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™, which absorb better when consumed with dietary fat. Some people tolerate vitamins without food, but absorption improves with a meal.

**💡 Pro Tip:** If morning nausea is an issue, try taking your multivitamin with lunch or dinner instead.

### **Are Multivitamins Vegan?**

**Some are.** Of the three multivitamins compared above, only Seed’s DM-02™ is vegan, while the other two are vegetarian.

Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™ is vegetarian and typically suitable for vegans, though checking the specific bottle for capsule coating details is recommended. Metagenics PhytoMulti® is generally vegetarian, but coating ingredients can vary by formulation.

### **What Is the Difference Between Seed DM-02 and Other Multivitamins?**

**DM-02™ is designed as a 2-in-1 multivitamin and prebiotic.** While other premium multivitamins like Metagenics PhytoMulti® and Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™ focus on bioavailable nutrients for systemic absorption, DM-02™ uses ViaCap® technology to deliver specific nutrients to the colon, supporting the gut microbiome.

It also includes a **Cellular Energy Complex (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine)** that most traditional multivitamins lack.

### **Which Multivitamin Is Best for Gut Health: Metagenics, Pure Encapsulations, or Seed?**

**Of the three compared here, only Seed DM-02™ is specifically designed with gut health in mind.**

Its ViaCap® capsule-in-capsule delivery system releases prebiotics and specific B vitamins directly to the colon to support the gut microbiome. Metagenics PhytoMulti® and Pure Encapsulations O.N.E.™ are high-quality multivitamins, but neither includes targeted microbiome support or prebiotic delivery technology.
