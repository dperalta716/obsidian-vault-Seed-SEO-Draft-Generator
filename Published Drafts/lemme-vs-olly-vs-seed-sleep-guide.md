## **What Makes a Sleep Supplement Actually Work?**

Before comparing bottles, it helps to understand what shapes whether a sleep supplement can realistically deliver on its claims.

### **Melatonin Dosing**

Spoiler alert: more isn’t better. In fact, “more” can sometimes be the problem.

Your brain naturally produces very little [melatonin](https://seed.com/cultured/melatonin-for-sleep-guide/)—typically around 0.1–0.5mg per night. Many supplements overload your system with 3mg, 5mg, or even 10mg. That’s 10 to 100 times what nature intended.

This massive spike can help you pass out, sure, but it often leads to vivid dreams, restless sleep, and that dreaded “melatonin hangover” the next day. (You know the one—where you technically slept but feel like you didn’t.)

### **Delivery Timing**

Sleep happens in phases. You need to fall asleep, but you also need to _stay_ asleep. A supplement that dumps all its ingredients into your system at once might help you doze off quickly, but it leaves you vulnerable to waking up at 3 a.m. staring at the clock.

### **The Gut-Brain-Sleep Axis**

This is the piece most people—and most products—miss.

Your gut and your brain are constantly texting each other. Emerging science shows that your gut microbiome plays a role in regulating your sleep-wake cycles. Your gut bacteria help produce and regulate neurotransmitters, including [GABA](https://seed.com/cultured/best-gaba-supplement-for-stress-guide/) and serotonin (the precursor to melatonin). A sleep supplement that considers this ecosystem may offer more comprehensive support. [1](https://doi.org/10.1152/physrev.00018.2018)

**🔬 Science Translation:** Your gut bacteria aren’t just digesting food—they’re helping manufacture the calming chemicals your brain needs to wind down. When your gut is out of balance, your sleep can suffer too.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **What’s the Best Melatonin Dose for Sleep?**

For most people, 0.3–0.5mg is enough. Your brain naturally produces around 0.1–0.5mg of melatonin per night, so doses of 3–10mg are far more than your body needs. Research suggests lower doses may be just as effective for falling asleep without causing next-day grogginess.

### **Why Is Seed’s Melatonin Dose (0.5mg) So Much Lower Than Lemme (5mg) or OLLY (3mg)?**

**Seed uses a “bioidentical” dose, meaning it matches the amount of melatonin your brain naturally produces each night (typically 0.1–0.5mg).** High doses like 3mg or 5mg can overwhelm your receptors, potentially causing grogginess and disrupting your natural production over time.

When comparing **Lemme Sleep vs. OLLY Sleep vs. Seed PM-02**, this dosing difference is one of the most significant distinctions.

### **Do Gummies Work as Well as Capsules for Sleep Supplements?**

**Yes, but with some caveats.** Gummies can effectively deliver sleep ingredients, but they typically contain added sugars and release everything at once. Capsules—especially those with extended-release formulas—can deliver ingredients gradually throughout the night, which may help you stay asleep rather than just fall asleep.

**💡 Pro Tip:** If you tend to wake up in the middle of the night, look for supplements with extended-release technology rather than immediate-release formats.

### **Can I Take Sleep Supplements Every Night?**

**Yes, most sleep supplements are non-habit forming and safe for nightly use.** But experts caution against long-term use of high-dose melatonin (5mg or more), as it may shift your circadian rhythm. If you’re taking sleep supplements regularly, look for lower doses closer to what your body naturally produces—around 0.3–0.5mg.

If you’re deciding between **Lemme Sleep vs. OLLY Sleep vs. Seed PM-02** for nightly use, consider how each dose aligns with your body’s natural melatonin production. (See more of this product comparison above. ⬆️)

### **Which Sleep Supplement Is Best for Sensitive Sleepers?**

**Low-dose melatonin supplements (0.5mg or less).** If you’re sensitive to melatonin or wake up groggy, higher doses (3–5mg) are more likely to cause next-day drowsiness. Extended-release formulas can also help by delivering melatonin gradually rather than all at once.
