Take a look at any multivitamin label and the names of included vitamins and minerals are similar across brands. But the format you choose—gummy or capsule—affects which nutrients can be included, what forms they come in, and how they survive digestion.

This guide compares Vitafusion’s Women’s Multivitamin, OLLY’s The Perfect Women’s Multi, and Seed’s DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin—reviewing each brand’s formulation, delivery, and price so you can choose a multivitamin based on what matters to you.

## **What Makes a Multivitamin Actually Work?**

Before getting into each product, it helps to understand what shapes whether a multivitamin can realistically deliver on its label claims.

### **Nutrient Forms and Absorption**

Not all vitamins are created equal—even when the label shows the same amount. “Bioavailability” refers to how easily your body can absorb and utilize a nutrient. **Many standard multivitamins use synthetic forms** (like folic acid or cyanocobalamin) because these forms are shelf-stable and affordable.

The tradeoff? Your body often has to convert synthetic forms into “active” versions before they can be used—a process that isn’t efficient for everyone, especially those with MTHFR gene variations. [1](https://doi.org/10.1097/MD.0000000000046564)

**🔬 Science Translation:** Think of synthetic vs. bioavailable nutrients like furniture that requires assembly vs. pre-assembled furniture. Both work—but one requires an extra step. Most people handle that assembly fine, though some (like those with MTHFR variations) benefit from the ready-to-use version.

### **Delivery**

Where nutrients end up in your [digestive system](https://seed.com/cultured/digestion-101/) matters as much as what’s in the bottle. Gummy vitamins use a gelatin or pectin matrix, which typically requires added sugar to mask the metallic taste of certain vitamins. (Have you ever tasted unsweetened vitamin C powder? Imagine that in a gummy. 😬)

Capsule technology, on the other hand, can be engineered to help nutrients survive stomach acid and reach the small intestine or colon—where absorption is most effective.

### **The Microbiome Factor: Does It Matter?**

Nutrition science has shifted in recent years. Researchers used to focus primarily on how vitamins and minerals support human cells. Now, there’s growing recognition that your gut microbiome plays a role too.

Though the extent of its contribution to overall vitamin status is still being studied, scientists _do_ know that certain gut bacteria can produce some B vitamins and vitamin K on their own—but they need the right inputs to do so. [2](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148), [3](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmed.2022.829304)

A multivitamin that considers this ecosystem is likely to deliver nutrients that support _both_ your cells _and_ your resident microbes.

## **A Closer Look: Seed’s DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin**

### **The 2-in-1 Difference: Nutrients for You and Your Microbiome**

Seed approaches the multivitamin as a system that supports whole-body health, starting with the gut. DM-02™ is a 2-in-1 formula: it includes 20 key vitamins and minerals for your body, plus a targeted blend designed to support your gut microbiome. (Yes, the microbes in your gut have nutritional needs too.)

### **Key DM-02™ Features and Benefit Claims**

DM-02™ is formulated to provide 100% **Daily Value (DV)** of all the nutrients in its formulation—enough to address common nutrition gaps **without overdoing it with megadoses.**(Many labels note up to 5,000% of a vitamin’s DV, which isn’t exactly useful, as your body excretes the excess. 🚽) Rather than overwhelming your system, this measured-dosing strategy focuses on what most adults actually need.

The DM-02™ formula targets several areas in the body, from the heart and immune system to [skin health](https://seed.com/cultured/best-vitamins-for-skin-guide/) and gut barrier integrity.

### **Bioavailability: Nutrient Forms Your Body Can Use**

Seed formulates DM-02™ with nutrient forms designed for direct absorption. Instead of using folic acid, which requires conversion, DM-02™ has **methylated folate (5-MTHF)**—the form your cells use. Instead of cyanocobalamin, it uses methylcobalamin and adenosylcobalamin for Vitamin B12. The formula also includes chelated minerals, which bind to amino acids for easier absorption. [4](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodchem.2021.131394)

These choices matter beyond personal absorption. Research suggests that the micronutrients you consume can also influence the composition of your gut microbiome. [5](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19010037) Your beneficial gut microbes rely on these nutrients just as much as [your brain](https://seed.com/cultured/vitamins-and-minerals-for-brain-health-guide/) and muscles do—making bioavailability a factor for your whole internal ecosystem.

### **Coenzyme Q10, Pyrroloquinoline Quinone, and Spermidine**

DM-02™ differs from standard gummy multivitamins by including a proprietary [“Cellular Energy Complex”](https://seed.com/cultured/your-cells-dont-need-hype-they-need-help/) containing CoQ10, PQQ, and spermidine—three compounds that aren’t found in most daily multivitamins.

- **Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10):** A compound that [supports mitochondrial health and function](https://seed.com/cultured/a-scientists-guide-to-the-best-mitochondria-supplement-guide/) but naturally declines as we age. [6](https://doi.org/10.3390/biology8020028)
- **Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ):** A micronutrient studied for its role in mitochondrial biogenesis (the creation of new mitochondria in your cells). [7](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M109.030130), [8](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.crfs.2024.100889)
- **Spermidine:** A polyamine (a type of organic compound) that supports autophagy—your body’s built-in system for recycling damaged cell parts. Autophagy becomes less efficient as we age, so supporting it may help maintain healthy cellular function over time. [9](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41556-024-01468-x)

**💡 Pro Tip:** CoQ10, PQQ, and spermidine are usually sold as separate (and expensive) standalone supplements. Getting all three in your daily multivitamin means fewer bottles and a more streamlined routine.

### **The Delivery System: ViaCap® Technology**

DM-02™ uses Seed’s ViaCap® delivery technology—a capsule-in-capsule system with two release points. The outer capsule dissolves in the upper GI tract, releasing vitamins and minerals for absorption in the small intestine. The inner capsule is formulated to resist stomach acid and deliver specific nutrients and [prebiotics](https://seed.com/cultured/can-you-take-prebiotics-and-probiotics-together-guide/) (like Japanese Wasabi extract) to the lower gut, where they can support the gut microbiome.

**💊 The intent behind this design:** certain nutrients and prebiotic compounds are sensitive to stomach acid. Staged delivery allows the formula to include ingredients that wouldn’t survive a single-capsule format.

### **Microbiome Support: The Gut-Nutrition Connection**

Ever wonder why some people seem to thrive on the same diet that leaves others [feeling sluggish](https://seed.com/cultured/vitamins-for-energy-for-women-guide/)? Part of the answer might be in their microbiome. Your gut bacteria have their own nutritional requirements, and DM-02™ is formulated with this in mind.

The inner capsule delivers targeted B vitamins and prebiotic compounds to the colon, providing fuel for beneficial bacteria, or [probiotics](https://seed.com/cultured/what-are-probiotics-benefits-strains-types-guide/). Those bacteria, in turn, can produce additional vitamins and **short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)** that support your gut lining and overall health.

### **Considerations for Seed’s Multivitamin**

DM-02™ is a comprehensive option that combines a multivitamin with microbiome support and cellular energy compounds in a single capsule.

The trade-offs are a higher price point ($39.99/month), a capsule format that won’t work for those who struggle to swallow pills, and no iron—which means pre-menopausal women with higher iron needs would need to supplement separately.

## **A Closer Look: Vitafusion Women’s Multivitamin**

### **Formulation Overview**

Vitafusion is one of the most recognized names in the gummy vitamin space. Their Women’s Multivitamin is designed as a “fusion of taste and nutrition” — an accessible option for people who want daily vitamin coverage without swallowing pills. (And yes, plenty of adults still struggle with that, and others just enjoy the taste—no judgment here! 😀)

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

Vitafusion Women’s Multivitamin includes Vitamins A, C, D, E, and B-vitamins. Because gummy vitamins require ingredients that remain stable in a sugary, moist environment, this product uses Folic Acid (synthetic) rather than Methylfolate, and Cyanocobalamin for B12—forms that require conversion in the body before they can be utilized.

### **Benefit Claims**

Vitafusion’s multivitamin for women is positioned to support energy metabolism, [bone health](https://seed.com/cultured/bone-health-supplements-guide/), and immune function. It’s designed for women who want reliable vitamin and nutrient coverage every day without needing a complicated routine.

Because for many people, having _something_ is better than having _nothing_—and Vitafusion makes that easy. ☑️

### **Delivery Format**

Vitafusion Women’s Multivitamin comes as berry-flavored gummies. The gummy format requires binding agents like glucose syrup and gelatin to hold the shape and mask vitamin taste. While it may taste pleasant, this means you’re consuming added sugar (typically 3g per serving) along with your vitamins.

The gummy format also limits what can be included—certain minerals and nutrient forms simply don’t remain stable in the chewy, sweet matrix.

### **Considerations for Vitafusion’s Multivitamin**

If you have trouble swallowing pills, Vitafusion offers a user-friendly alternative by using a gummy format. The familiar candy-like experience also makes it easier to remember your daily dose.

The trade-off is the use of synthetic vitamin forms and the inclusion of added sugar and gelatin. If you’re vegetarian, pregnant, or specifically looking for methylated B vitamins, Vitafusion may not meet your needs.

## **How to Choose the Right Multivitamin: Seed, OLLY, or Vitafusion?**

### **Choose Vitafusion or OLLY If:**

- You **prefer gummy vitamins** and find the sugar and flavor help you stay consistent with your daily routine. (Just keep in mind that there are tradeoffs in terms of nutrient forms and what can be included.)
- You physically **cannot swallow pills or capsules**. The gummy format of OLLY and Vitafusion is the primary advantage here.
- **Budget** is the main factor driving your decision. Vitafusion is the most affordable of the three options, while OLLY is a mid-range option.

### **Choose Seed’s DM-02™ If:**

- You want **nutrient forms designed for better absorption and utilization**. DM-02™ includes methylated folate and chelated minerals.
- You want **comprehensive support**. The inclusion of the Cellular Energy Complex (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine) adds value that usually requires buying separate supplements.
- You want to **avoid added sugars**. DM-02™ delivers vitamins and nutrients without glucose syrup, gelatin, or artificial flavors.
- You’d like to [**support your gut health**](https://seed.com/cultured/seed-vs-other-probiotics-for-gut-health/) alongside your basic vitamin needs. DM-02™ is formulated with the understanding that nutrition and the microbiome are linked.

## **The Key Insight**

Not all multivitamins are built the same way—their format determines what can go in the formula. On the one hand, gummy vitamins require taste-masking ingredients like sugar and a stable matrix, which limits certain minerals and nutrient forms. On the other hand, capsules allow for different ingredient options—including compounds that wouldn’t survive in a gummy—but they’re not the right fit for everyone.

**Multivitamins like Vitafusion and OLLY offer accessibility:** familiar formats, lower price points, and no pills to swallow. For people who struggle with capsules or want a simple daily habit without much friction, these products fill that role.

**Seed’s DM-02™ prioritizes bioavailable nutrient forms and includes beneficial compounds** (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine) typically sold separately. The capsule-in-capsule design allows for targeted delivery, though the tradeoff is a higher price point and a format that won’t work for everyone.

**The “best” multivitamin** depends on what you’re optimizing for — consistency, budget, nutrient forms, or additional functional ingredients. None of these products is universally right or wrong.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **Why Doesn’t My Multivitamin Have Iron?**

**For gummy vitamins, iron has a strong, metallic taste that is very difficult to mask.** It can also cause stomach upset for some people. Because gummies are designed around taste and texture, most gummy brands—including Vitafusion and OLLY—leave iron out.

Seed’s DM-02™ multivitamin is _not_ a gummy, but also excludes iron for a different reason: iron can interfere with the absorption of other minerals in the formula. Additionally, DM-02™ was designed for the general adult population, and most men and postmenopausal women don’t need supplemental iron—excess intake can actually be harmful.

⚕️ _If you have a specific iron deficiency, a standalone iron supplement taken separately from your multivitamin is generally the better approach. Talk to your doctor if this is a concern for you._

### **Is the Sugar in Gummy Vitamins a Problem?**

**It depends on your goals.** While 2-3 grams of sugar isn’t a massive amount (it’s less than a teaspoon), it is still added sugar that you’re consuming daily.

For some people, the bigger issue is that the sugar and gelatin matrix takes up space in the gummy that could otherwise be used for more nutrients, limiting how comprehensive the formula can be.

### **Can I Take Seed DM-02™ If I Have the MTHFR Gene Variation?**

**Yes—DM-02™ is formulated with methylated B vitamins specifically for this reason.** Many people with MTHFR variations look for methylated forms of B vitamins because their bodies struggle to convert synthetic folic acid.

DM-02™ includes **methylated folate (5-MTHF)** and **methylcobalamin (B12)**, which are forms that don’t require the same conversion process as the synthetic forms found in most gummy multivitamins.
