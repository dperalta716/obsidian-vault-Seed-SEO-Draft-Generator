Take a look at any multivitamin label and the names of included vitamins and minerals look similar across brands. But the format you choose—gummy or capsule—affects which nutrients can be included, what forms they come in, and how they survive digestion. 🔬

This guide compares SmartyPants, OLLY, and Seed’s DM-02™ on the factors that actually affect how well a multivitamin works: nutrient forms, delivery, sugar content, and microbiome support.

## **What Makes a Multivitamin Actually Work?**

Before getting into each product, it helps to understand what shapes whether a multivitamin can realistically deliver on its label claims.

### **Bioavailability: Can Your Body Use What You’re Taking?**

Not all vitamins are created equal—even when the label shows the same amount. “Bioavailability” refers to how easily your body can absorb and utilize a nutrient. Many standard multivitamins use synthetic forms (like folic acid or cyanocobalamin) because they’re cheaper and more shelf-stable.

The tradeoff? Your body has to convert synthetic forms into “active” versions before they can be used. Some research suggests over 30% of the global population has a genetic variation (MTHFR) that makes this conversion less efficient. [1](https://doi.org/10.3390/genes16040441)

**🔬 Science Translation:** Think of synthetic vs. bioavailable nutrients like furniture that requires assembly vs. pre-assembled furniture. Both work—but one requires an extra step. Most people handle that assembly fine, though some (like those with MTHFR variations) benefit from the ready-to-use version.

A multivitamin with bioavailable forms (methylfolate, methylcobalamin, chelated minerals) skips that conversion step entirely.

### **Delivery: Where Do the Nutrients Actually End Up?**

Where nutrients end up in your [digestive system](https://seed.com/cultured/digestion-101/) matters as much as what’s in the bottle. Your stomach is a pool of hydrochloric acid—not exactly a gentle environment. A standard capsule or gummy dissolves there, which means sensitive compounds can degrade before they ever reach the small intestine, where most absorption happens. [2](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.heliyon.2022.e12265)

Gummy vitamins face additional constraints: the gelatin or pectin matrix requires added sugar to mask the metallic taste of certain vitamins.

### **The Microbiome Factor: Does It Matter?**

Nutrition science has shifted in recent years. Researchers used to focus primarily on how vitamins and minerals support human cells. Now, there’s growing recognition that your gut microbiome plays a role too.

Your gut bacteria have nutritional needs—and they actually produce certain B vitamins and vitamin K on their own, given the right inputs. [3](https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2015.00148), [4](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmed.2022.829304)

A multivitamin that considers this ecosystem—rather than just your human cells—takes a more complete approach to nutrition.

## **A Closer Look: Seed’s DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin**

Seed approaches the multivitamin as a system that supports whole-body health, starting with the gut.

### **The 2-in-1 Design: Nutrients for You and Your Microbiome**

DM-02™ is a 2-in-1 formulation. The outer capsule delivers 20 essential vitamins and minerals to your body, while the inner capsule delivers specific nutrients and prebiotics to your microbiome. (Yes, the microbes in your gut have nutritional needs too.)

Growing research shows that nutrition works both ways: healthy microbes may support nutrient absorption, and the right nutrients may support microbial health.

**Key DM-02™ features include:**

- **20 Essential Nutrients:** Vitamins A, C, D3, E, K (both K1 and K2), plus B-complex vitamins and essential minerals
- **100% Daily Value:** Each nutrient is dosed at 100% DV
- **Single Capsule:** Taken daily with or without food

### **Bioavailable Nutrient Forms**

Seed formulates its supplements with active forms of nutrients your body can absorb and use directly:

- **Methylfolate (5-MTHF)** instead of folic acid
- **Methylcobalamin** for B12 (plus adenosylcobalamin)
- **Vitamin K2 (MK-7)** alongside K1, which research suggests may support [bone health](https://seed.com/cultured/bone-health-supplements-guide/) and [cardiovascular health](https://seed.com/cultured/heart-health-supplements-cardiovascular-guide/). [5](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8483258/)
- **Chelated minerals** (like manganese bisglycinate), which are bound to amino acids to support absorption through the gut wall. [6](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodchem.2021.131394)

Research also suggests that bioavailable micronutrients may positively influence the composition of the gut microbiome. [7](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9311823/)

### **The Cellular Energy Complex**

Most multivitamins stop at the standard A-through-Zinc lineup. DM-02™ includes a proprietary complex designed to [support healthy mitochondrial function](https://seed.com/cultured/a-scientists-guide-to-the-best-mitochondria-supplement-guide/)—the energy-producing structures in your cells:

- **CoQ10:** A compound that [supports cellular energy production](https://seed.com/cultured/your-cells-dont-need-hype-they-need-help/) but naturally declines as we age. [8](https://doi.org/10.3390/biology8020028)
- **PQQ:** A micronutrient studied for its ability to stimulate mitochondrial biogenesis—the growth of new mitochondria. [9](https://doi.org/10.1074/jbc.M109.030130)
- **Spermidine:** A polyamine that supports autophagy, your body’s built-in cellular recycling system. [10](https://doi.org/10.14336/AD.2021.0603)

**💡 Pro Tip:** CoQ10, PQQ, and spermidine are usually sold as separate (and often expensive) standalone supplements. Getting all three in your daily multivitamin means fewer bottles and a more streamlined routine.

### **The Delivery System: ViaCap® Technology**

Seed uses their proprietary **ViaCap®** delivery technology—a capsule nested within a capsule. This design serves multiple purposes:

- **Stomach Acid Protection:** The outer capsule shields sensitive nutrients from degradation in the acidic stomach environment
- **Staged Release:** The inner capsule is formulated to resist stomach acid and release its contents later in the digestive tract, closer to the colon where the microbiome resides
- **Separation of Ingredients:** Some nutrients and prebiotic compounds are sensitive to stomach acid—staged delivery allows the formula to include ingredients that wouldn’t survive a single-capsule format

**💊 The intent behind this design:** getting nutrients _into_ your body is only part of the equation—getting them to the right _place_ matters just as much.

### **Microbiome Support**

DM-02™ contains a prebiotic complex derived from Japanese wasabi and pea starch. These aren’t fillers—they’re functional ingredients that serve as food for your gut bacteria.

The inner capsule delivers targeted B vitamins and prebiotic compounds to the colon, providing fuel for beneficial bacteria. Those bacteria, in turn, can produce additional vitamins and [**short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)** like butyrate](https://seed.com/cultured/what-is-butyrate-benefits-foods-guide/) that support your gut lining and overall health.

### **Considerations for Seed’s Multivitamin**

DM-02™ is a comprehensive option that combines a multivitamin with microbiome support and cellular energy compounds in a single capsule.

The trade-offs are a higher price point ($39.99/month), a capsule format that won’t work for those who struggle to swallow pills, and no iron—which means pre-menopausal women with higher iron needs would need to supplement separately.

## **A Closer Look: SmartyPants Women’s Multi & Omegas**

SmartyPants has positioned itself as a “premium” option in the gummy category, with more attention to ingredient quality than many competitors.

### **Formulation Overview**

SmartyPants distinguishes itself by including Omega-3 fatty acids (from fish oil or flax, depending on the formula) directly in the gummy. This “all-in-one” approach aims to consolidate your fish oil and multivitamin into a single product.

The formula covers a broad range of nutrients and is designed as a comprehensive daily supplement rather than a bare-minimum approach.

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

SmartyPants uses methylfolate and **methylcobalamin (B12)**—the same bioavailable forms found in capsule-based formulas. For a gummy vitamin, this is notable.

They also include Vitamin D3. Some formulas (like the Organic Women’s Multi) contain **Vitamin K2 (MK-7)**, though not all of the products in their lineup include it—so check the specific formula for the product you choose.

### **SmartyPants: Multivitamin Benefit Claims**

SmartyPants positions itself as a comprehensive solution: “Multivitamin + Omega-3s + Vitamin D.” The goal is to replace multiple bottles with a single daily habit.

For people who want bioavailable nutrient forms but prefer gummies over capsules, this addresses a real gap in the market. The inclusion of Omega-3s is genuinely convenient for those who don’t want to take a separate fish oil supplement.

### **Delivery Format**

The gummy format is both the appeal and the limitation. To fit all those nutrients and Omega-3s into a gummy _and_ make it taste acceptable, the serving size is larger—typically 3-4 gummies per day depending on the specific product.

Gummies also have inherent constraints: certain minerals (like iron) are difficult to include, and heat-sensitive compounds may be less stable than in capsule form.

**⚛️ Fun Fact:** Gummy vitamins were originally developed for children who couldn’t swallow pills. The adult gummy market exploded in the 2010s when supplement companies realized grown-ups wanted the same tasty convenience.

### **Considerations for SmartyPants Multivitamin**

SmartyPants offers something uncommon in the gummy category: bioavailable forms like methylfolate and methylcobalamin, plus built-in Omega-3s. For people who prefer gummies but want higher-quality nutrient forms, this fills a real gap. The tasty format and wide retail availability also support daily habit formation.

The trade-offs are added sugar (up to 7g per serving, though a zero-sugar option exists), a larger serving size (3-4 gummies daily), and a higher price point than basic gummies (~$30/month). Like most gummies, it does not contain iron or microbiome-supporting ingredients.

## **A Closer Look: OLLY The Perfect Women’s Multi**

[OLLY](https://seed.com/cultured/hum-nutrition-vs-olly-vs-seed-dm-02-guide/) is an accessible, approachable option. Their colorful bottles and playful branding have made vitamins feel less like medicine and more like self-care.

### **Formulation Overview**

OLLY focuses on the fundamentals. “The Perfect Women’s Multi” covers 18 nutrients designed to address common gaps in a standard diet. The philosophy is straightforward: provide reliable daily coverage without overcomplicating things.

For someone who has never taken a multivitamin before—or who has struggled to stay consistent—this simplicity can be a genuine advantage. Sometimes the best vitamin is the one you actually take. (And yes, plenty of adults still struggle with pill-swallowing—no judgment here! 😀)

### **Key Ingredients and Forms**

To keep the price accessible and the taste consistent, OLLY uses standard nutrient forms. For example, they use **folic acid** rather than methylfolate, and cyanocobalamin for B12.

For many people, this works fine—the body can convert these forms into the active versions it needs. But for those with MTHFR variations (which may affect conversion efficiency), the bioavailable forms aren’t included here.

Like SmartyPants and Seed, OLLY’s Women’s Multi does not contain iron.

### **OLLY’s Benefit Claims**

OLLY’s multivitamin for women is positioned to support energy, [skin](https://seed.com/cultured/best-vitamins-for-skin-guide/), and bone health. It’s designed as a daily vitamin “insurance policy” for covering nutritional gaps—not as a high-performance formula with advanced ingredients.

The brand emphasizes [biotin for hair, skin, and nails](https://seed.com/cultured/biotin-for-hair-and-nails-guide/). The branding is aspirational and lifestyle-forward, which may appeal more to women who want their supplements to feel like self-care rather than a chore. 💅

### **Delivery Format**

Convenience is the priority. The serving size is just **2 gummies daily**—easier to remember and less to chew than competitors requiring 3-4+ gummies.

The Blissful Berry flavor is a fan favorite, and for people who genuinely struggle with pill-swallowing, this format removes a real barrier to consistency. OLLY’s formula contains glucose syrup and beet sugar, with around 2g of added sugar per serving.

### **Considerations for OLLY’s Multivitamin**

If you have trouble swallowing pills, OLLY offers a user-friendly alternative with its gummy format. The familiar candy-like experience, small serving size (just 2 gummies), and wide retail availability make it easy to build and maintain a daily habit. At ~$14/month, it’s also the most affordable option in this comparison.

The trade-off is the use of synthetic vitamin forms (folic acid, cyanocobalamin) and the inclusion of added sugar. If you’re specifically looking for bioavailable nutrient forms, cellular energy compounds, or microbiome support, OLLY may not meet those needs.

## **The Key Insight**

Not all multivitamins are built the same way—format determines what can go in the formula. Gummy vitamins require taste-masking ingredients like sugar and a stable matrix, which limits certain minerals and nutrient forms. Capsules allow for different ingredient options—including compounds that wouldn’t survive in a gummy—but they’re not the right fit for everyone.

**OLLY offers accessibility:** a familiar format, lower price point, and no pills to swallow. For people who struggle with capsules or want a simple daily habit without much friction, OLLY fills that role.

**SmartyPants bridges the gap** between gummy convenience and higher-quality nutrient forms. The inclusion of methylfolate, methylcobalamin, and Omega-3s makes it a step up from basic gummies—though the trade-offs are added sugar and a larger serving size.

**Seed’s DM-02™ prioritizes bioavailable nutrient forms and includes compounds** (CoQ10, PQQ, spermidine) typically sold separately. The capsule-in-capsule design allows for targeted delivery to the microbiome, though the trade-off is a higher price point and a format that won’t work for everyone.

The “best” multivitamin depends on what you’re optimizing for—consistency, budget, nutrient forms, or additional functional ingredients. None of these products is universally right or wrong. 🌱

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **Do Gummy Multivitamins Contain Iron?**

**Most gummy multivitamins—including OLLY and SmartyPants—do not contain iron.** Iron has a strong, metallic taste that is very difficult to mask in gummy form. It can also cause stomach upset for some people.

Seed’s DM-02™ is not a gummy but also excludes iron for a different reason: iron can interfere with the absorption of other minerals in the formula. Additionally, DM-02™ was designed for the general adult population, and most men and postmenopausal women don’t need supplemental iron—excess intake can actually be harmful.

If your healthcare provider has identified an iron deficiency, a standalone iron supplement taken separately from your multivitamin is generally the better approach. This is mostly applicable to women of reproductive age. **🤓 Learn More:** [**The Best Multivitamin for Women in 2026**](https://seed.com/cultured/the-thinking-persons-guide-to-the-best-multivitamin-for-women-guide/)

### **Is the Sugar in Gummy Vitamins a Problem?**

**It depends on your goals.** SmartyPants contains up to 7g of added sugar per serving—that’s roughly one-third of the American Heart Association’s recommended daily limit for women (25g). Though they offer a zero-sugar option. OLLY contains about 2g per serving.

While these amounts aren’t dramatic in isolation, taken every single day they add up. For some people, the bigger issue is that the sugar and gelatin matrix takes up space in the gummy that could otherwise be used for more nutrients.

**💡 Pro Tip:** If you’re actively managing blood sugar or trying to minimize added sugars for metabolic or gut health, a sugar-free option like Seed’s DM-02™ may be preferable.

### **Can I Take Seed DM-02™ With an MTHFR Gene Variation?**

**Yes—this is one of DM-02™’s specific strengths.** It uses **methylfolate (5-MTHF)** and methylcobalamin, which are the active forms your body can utilize directly. This bypasses the conversion step that can be inefficient for those with MTHFR variations. [1](https://doi.org/10.3390/genes16040441)

SmartyPants also uses methylfolate. OLLY uses folic acid (the synthetic form that requires conversion).

### **Which Multivitamin Is Best for Gut Health?**

**Seed’s DM-02™ is the only option in this comparison specifically designed to support the microbiome.** Its ViaCap® delivery system targets the colon, and the [prebiotic complex](https://seed.com/cultured/how-long-do-prebiotics-take-to-work-guide/) (from Japanese wasabi and pea starch) provides fuel for beneficial gut bacteria.

Neither OLLY nor SmartyPants includes microbiome-targeted ingredients or delivery technology.

### **Why Does Seed Cost More Than Other Multivitamins?**

**The price reflects the formulation complexity.** DM-02™ includes bioavailable nutrient forms (which cost more than synthetic versions), a proprietary delivery system (ViaCap®), and additional compounds like CoQ10, PQQ, and spermidine that most multivitamins don’t include.

Whether that added value is worth the higher price depends on your priorities. If basic coverage is sufficient for your needs, OLLY at ~$14/month may be the better fit. If you want comprehensive support with additional functional ingredients, the investment in DM-02™ may make sense.
