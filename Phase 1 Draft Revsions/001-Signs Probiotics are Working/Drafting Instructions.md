### Addition #3: "What If Probiotics Aren't Working?" Section
**Placement**: NEW H2 section after "What probiotics should you choose?" and before FAQ

**Section Title**: `## What If You're Not Seeing Results?`

**Content Requirements** (200-250 words):

Opening: Normalize the experience
- "You've been taking your probiotics consistently for 8+ weeks, and... nothing. What gives?"

**Subheading**: Common reasons probiotics might not be working for you

1. **Wrong strains for your goals** (50 words)
   - Different strains do different things
   - Example: Strain studied for IBS won't necessarily help with skin
   - "It's like expecting a screwdriver to work as a hammer"
   - Suggestion: Look for products with strains studied for your specific concern

2. **Storage or viability issues** (50 words)
   - Heat, moisture, light degrade probiotics
   - Expired products may have zero live bacteria
   - Signs your probiotics might be dead: no effect after 8 weeks, bottle stored improperly
   - Tip: Choose shelf-stable options or refrigerate properly

3. **Your diet is working against you** (50-75 words)
   - Low fiber diet means probiotics have nothing to eat (no prebiotics)
   - High sugar/processed food feeds harmful bacteria
   - Alcohol and antibiotics disrupt microbiome
   - "Think of it like planting a garden in concrete—even the best seeds won't grow"
   - Suggestion: Add prebiotic-rich foods (vegetables, whole grains, legumes)

4. **Unrealistic expectations** (50 words)
   - Probiotics aren't magic pills
   - They support gut health, but don't cure diseases
   - Work best as part of overall healthy lifestyle
   - "If you're looking for a single-supplement solution to complex health issues, you'll likely be disappointed"

Closing: When to try something different
- If none of the above apply, might need different strains or multi-strain formula
- Consider working with healthcare provider or dietitian

- 0-1 on strain specificity or prebiotic importance
- Most of this is practical guidance, doesn't need heavy citation

**Tone Reminders**:
- Empathetic, not discouraging

The reality:
- Fermented foods ARE beneficial and contain probiotics
- But: CFU counts are unpredictable, strains aren't standardized
- Yogurt might have millions of bacteria, but specific supplement strains are studied for specific effects

- If you're generally healthy with no specific concerns
- Just want to maintain good gut health
- Eating fermented foods daily

When supplements make more sense:
- Specific health goals (IBS, post-antibiotic recovery, immune support)
- Want consistent, studied strains
- Don't regularly eat fermented foods
