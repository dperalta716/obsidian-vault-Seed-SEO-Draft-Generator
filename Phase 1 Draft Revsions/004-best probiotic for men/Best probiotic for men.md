## **Probiotic Strains That Support Digestive Health**

### **Support for Staying Regular** 

Let's talk about strains that have actually been studied for helping keep things moving smoothly. üí©

* ***Bifidobacterium longum*** **BB536:** This multifunctional strain has been shown to support healthy bowel regularity and promote overall gastrointestinal health.[2](http://doi.org/10.1016/j.jff.2019.02.002)

* ***L. plantarum*** **LP01 and *B. breve* BR03:** A review showed that this strain mixture helps with stool consistency and ease of passage.[3](http://doi.org/10.1097/MCG.0b013e3181ee31c3)

### **Reinforcing the Gut's Protective Wall**

Your gut lining works like a finely tuned filter: allowing nutrients through while blocking out things that don‚Äôt belong. But over time, factors like stress, aging, and diet can wear down this filter, making it easier for unwanted substances to pass through.[4](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tem.2022.01.002)

This kind of tight junction support isn't exclusive to men, but it may be especially relevant if you're noticing increased sensitivity or digestive disruptions. Just make sure the full strain name is listed on the label, not just the species, since benefits are strain-specific.[7](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmed.2018.00124)

Research has shown that the gut microbiome changes as we age‚Äîand not necessarily in helpful ways.[8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41435-021-00126-8),[9](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-genet-112618-043650) Think of it like a system that‚Äôs been running smoothly for years, but now needs a bit more maintenance to perform at its best.

### **Strain Specificity vs. Generic Claims**

Not all probiotics are the same, and the specific strain determines the benefits you'll receive.[1](http://doi.org/10.1038/nrgastro.2014.66),[7](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmed.2018.00124) It's like how "dog" tells you almost *nothing* about what you're getting‚Äîis it a Great Dane or a Chihuahua? Both are dogs, but the experience is wildly different. (If you know, you know.)

Many products list only general bacteria types like "*Lactobacillus* *acidophilus*" without specifying the strain. Without this information, it's impossible to know if you're getting the studied version or its less-impressive cousin.[7](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmed.2018.00124) 

Look for products that clearly list **complete strain designations** (like *Bifidobacterium longum* BB536 or *Lactobacillus acidophilus* NCFM) and provide references to clinical studies. This level of transparency typically indicates a company that cares about science, not just marketing.

### **Clinically Studied Doses**

For probiotics to be effective, they need to contain enough beneficial bacteria to make a difference.[22](https://doi.org/10.3920/bm2016.0140) It's like coffee‚Äîone drop in a gallon of water technically contains caffeine, but you won't feel the effects. 

Some products list CFU ([colony-forming units](https://seed.com/cultured/cfu-vs-afu-whats-the-difference-and-which-is-better/)), but **AFU (active fluorescent units)** provide a more precise count by capturing all viable cells, not just those that grow on traditional culture plates in a lab.[23](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2022.989563)

### **Delivery Technology and Viability**

[Probiotics work optimally](https://seed.com/cultured/do-probiotics-work-benefits-and-strains/)  when they reach your intestines alive. Many probiotic organisms are killed by stomach acid before they ever reach their destination‚Äîlike sending troops into battle without armor. 

Look for products with technology designed to protect probiotic organisms through digestion, such as:

* Acid-resistant capsules [24](http://doi.org/10.3390/microorganisms11010095) 

* Lyophilization [25](http://doi.org/10.3390/applmicrobiol3040092) 

* Shelf stability (because your probiotic shouldn‚Äôt need more chilling than your leftovers.) [25](http://doi.org/10.3390/applmicrobiol3040092),[26](http://doi.org/10.1093/femsmc/xtaa007)

### **Quality Testing and Transparency** 

The probiotic market is surprisingly under-regulated, with many products failing to contain what they claim.[1](http://doi.org/10.1038/nrgastro.2014.66) 

**A quality probiotic company should:**

* Test their products throughout shelf life (not just at manufacturing) [26](http://doi.org/10.1093/femsmc/xtaa007)

* Be transparent about testing methods

* Provide clear information about specific strains and benefits 

* [Share research supporting their claims](https://seed.com/cultured/how-seed-is-fixing-probiotic-research/)

üîé In other words, look for a company that **display they have nothing to hide**‚Äîbecause they don‚Äôt.

## **How to Incorporate Probiotics into Your Daily Routine**

For probiotics to be effective, consistency matters more than perfect timing or elaborate rituals.

* **Consistency:** The most important factor is taking your probiotic regularly, so choose a time that fits easily into your routine. (Morning bathroom visit? Evening tooth-brushing? Pick something you already do daily.)

* **During Antibiotic Treatment:** If you're [taking antibiotics](https://seed.com/cultured/antibiotics-and-good-bacteria-guide/), your gut microbiome can be adversely affected.[27](http://doi.org/10.3389/fcimb.2020.572912) Probiotics may help‚Äîit's like sending in the cleanup crew after the demolition team has left.[27](http://doi.org/10.3389/fcimb.2020.572912) It may be wise to space your probiotic intake by at least 2-3 hours to give the beneficial bacteria a better chance at survival.[28](http://doi.org/10.2146/ajhp090168)
