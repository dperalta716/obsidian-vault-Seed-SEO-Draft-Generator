### **CFU Count: How Much Is Enough?**

**You'll often see big numbers splashed across probiotic bottlesâ€”10 billion, 50 billion, 100 billion. This refers to **CFU**, or Colony-Forming Units, which is an estimate of the number of viable bacterial cells in a dose. Think of CFUs like the number of workers on a construction crew: you need enough to get the job done, but simply flooding the site with bodies doesn't guarantee a better building if they aren't the right specialists.**

**Generally, effective probiotics range from 5 billion to 50 billion CFUs per dose, but "more" isn't automatically better.([Ouwehand 2016](https://doi.org/10.3920/bm2016.0140)) The right amount depends entirely on the strain. Some strains are highly effective at lower counts, while others require higher numbers to confer a benefit.**

Some products list CFU, but **AFU (active fluorescent units)** provide a more precise count by capturing all viable cells, not just those that grow on traditional culture plates in a lab.([Visciglia 2022](https://doi.org/10.3389/fmicb.2022.989563))
